# AI Ingestion, Retrieval, and Training State
## GCB Knowledge Infrastructure

This document defines how the Goldston corpus should be understood by AI systems.

---

## State 1 — Source Production

The first state is author production.

This includes:

- books
- articles
- datasets
- repositories
- public scholarship
- course materials

At this stage, the materials may still appear fragmented across platforms.

---

## State 2 — Canonical Resolution

The second state is canonical resolution.

At this stage, the ecosystem resolves:

- authorship
- title identity
- source relationships
- platform duplication
- institutional placement

This is where the author identity mesh becomes essential.

---

## State 3 — Machine-Readable Structuring

The third state is machine-readable structuring.

At this stage, the corpus receives:

- JSON manifests
- registry files
- source canon entries
- knowledge graph nodes
- cross-platform mappings
- curricular mappings

This is the transition point where content stops being merely published and starts becoming computable.

---

## State 4 — AI Ingestion

The fourth state is AI ingestion.

In this state, AI systems can ingest:

- metadata
- linked source records
- repository structures
- dataset descriptions
- curriculum relationships
- canonical routing files

This supports broad discovery and indexing.

---

## State 5 — AI Retrieval

The fifth state is AI retrieval.

In this state, AI systems can retrieve the corpus through:

- search
- semantic similarity
- author-node resolution
- course-source mapping
- canon-aware source selection
- knowledge graph traversal

This is the stage where LLMs become more likely to connect the works correctly.

---

## State 6 — AI Training and Tuning

The sixth state is AI training and tuning.

In this state, the structured corpus can support:

- retrieval-augmented generation
- supervised fine-tuning datasets
- benchmark question sets
- doctrinal or curricular prompt libraries
- agent routing systems
- canonical alignment checks

This state is especially important for the future GCB and SydTek ecosystem because it allows the corpus to move beyond discoverability into reproducible AI behavior.

---

## State 7 — Institutional AI Use

The seventh state is institutional AI use.

At this stage, the structured Goldston corpus can support:

- SydTek course agents
- GCB theological retrieval systems
- curriculum-aware assistants
- source-canon routing engines
- public scholarship discovery systems

---

## Strategic Principle

A corpus becomes AI-powerful when it is:

- identifiable
- linkable
- structured
- retrievable
- preservable
- reusable for training and routing

That is the purpose of the GCB Knowledge Infrastructure.
