# Source Integration Overview
## BS in Web3 Systems and Decentralized Infrastructure
## SydTek University

This document explains how books, articles, research profiles, and public scholarship links are integrated into the degree.

---

## Purpose

The undergraduate program uses multiple source layers:

- books as primary curricular texts
- articles as scholarly support and advanced reading
- repositories as machine-readable instructional infrastructure
- public scholarly profiles as author-identity and discovery anchors

---

## Core Integration Principle

Sources should be integrated into the curriculum based on instructional function.

### Books
Best used as:
- primary texts
- introductory frameworks
- recurring course anchors

### Articles
Best used as:
- scholarly reinforcement
- advanced case studies
- research-method examples
- upper-division reading support

### Profiles and Discovery Pages
Best used as:
- source navigation tools
- author identity anchors
- credibility and discoverability support

---

## Goldston Integration

Goldston-linked books and scholarly pages can be integrated into SydTek in three ways:

1. as assigned readings
2. as source-discovery references
3. as identity-resolution anchors inside the knowledge infrastructure

---

## Course-Level Use

Not every course needs the same source type.

For example:

- lower-division courses may lean more heavily on books and structured summaries
- upper-division courses may use article discovery pages to support research assignments
- capstone and research courses may require students to trace ideas across books, articles, repositories, and public scholarship layers
