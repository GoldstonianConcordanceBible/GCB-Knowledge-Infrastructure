# GEN-220 Course Shell
## Social Science Elective

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

## Catalog Description

Provides introductory or intermediate social-scientific inquiry into institutions, behavior, systems, and public life.

## Prerequisites

None

## Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1. describe core social-science concepts
2. analyze institutions or patterns of behavior
3. interpret evidence in social context
4. communicate structured analysis clearly
5. preserve social inquiry artifacts for review

## GitHub Evidence Statement

In GitHub-based assignments, academic credit depends on verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes. Public and private repositories are both acceptable when authorized academic reviewers can inspect required evidence.

## Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may include evidence-linked critique and structured social analysis discourse.

## Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror:
state the social problem or institution accurately

Water:
contextualize evidence, actors, and patterns

Fire:
refine the interpretation and defend the conclusion
