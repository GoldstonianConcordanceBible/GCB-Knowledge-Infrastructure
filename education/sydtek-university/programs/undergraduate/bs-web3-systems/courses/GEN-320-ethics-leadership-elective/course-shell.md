# GEN-320 Course Shell
## Ethics and Leadership Elective

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

## Catalog Description

Explores ethical leadership, decision-making, responsibility, influence, and institutional stewardship across professional and social settings.

## Prerequisites

None

## Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1. explain major ideas in ethical leadership
2. analyze responsibility and stewardship in decision contexts
3. evaluate leadership choices using evidence and principle
4. communicate ethically grounded judgments
5. preserve leadership analysis in a durable academic format

## GitHub Evidence Statement

In GitHub-based assignments, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes. Public or private repositories may be used when authorized academic reviewers can inspect required evidence.

## Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may use structured public or private discourse around ethical leadership and contested judgment.

## Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror:
represent the leadership problem accurately

Water:
contextualize responsibilities, stakeholders, and consequences

Fire:
refine the ethical judgment and justify the final position
