# WEB3-110
## Internet Evolution: Web1, Web2, and Web3
## SydTek University

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate  
Program: BS in Web3 Systems and Decentralized Infrastructure

---

## Course Description

This course examines the evolution of the internet from early static publishing environments to platform-centered systems and emerging decentralized infrastructures.

Students analyze how changes in architecture, ownership, participation, and control shaped the movement from Web1 to Web2 and toward Web3.

The course also develops students’ ability to compare technological eras and explain how institutional, social, and economic forces influenced internet design.

---

## Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. describe key differences among Web1, Web2, and Web3
2. explain how platform models changed digital participation and control
3. analyze shifts in ownership, governance, and data power
4. compare centralized and decentralized internet structures
5. preserve evidence-linked course work in a reviewable format
