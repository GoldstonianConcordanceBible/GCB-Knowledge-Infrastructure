# WEB3-430 Course Shell
## Governance Lab

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

## Catalog Description

Lab-based course focused on governance design, proposal systems, treasury logic, stakeholder structure, and applied critique of decentralized organizational models.

## Prerequisites

WEB3-340  
program approval recommended

## Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1. analyze governance structures in applied settings
2. design and test governance workflows
3. document proposal, treasury, and participation logic
4. revise governance models based on critique
5. preserve governance-lab evidence in a reviewable format

## GitHub Evidence Statement

This course may use GitHub, linked governance artifacts, or other version-controlled systems as primary evidence environments.

Academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes. Public and private repositories are both acceptable when authorized academic reviewers can inspect required evidence.

## Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This lab may include public or private critique of governance models, proposal drafts, and organizational logic.

## Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror:
state the governance problem and structure accurately

Water:
contextualize stakeholders, processes, and tradeoffs

Fire:
revise, test, and defend the governance design
