# ENG-101
## Writing for Technical Systems
## SydTek University

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate  
Program: BS in Web3 Systems and Decentralized Infrastructure

---

## Course Description

This course develops clear, organized, and audience-aware writing for technical, academic, and documentation contexts.

Students learn how to explain systems, summarize sources, compare ideas, define terms precisely, and produce durable written artifacts suitable for academic review and technical use.

The course also introduces students to SydTek University’s evidence-linked learning approach by requiring organized written submissions, revision cycles, and, where applicable, GitHub-based documentation.

---

## Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. write clearly for academic and technical audiences
2. organize information logically and coherently
3. define and explain complex concepts in accessible language
4. revise written work based on feedback and reflection
5. preserve written artifacts in a documented and reviewable format

## GitHub Evidence Statement

In GitHub-based courses, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Credit is awarded for documented writing, revision, and progression, not for visibility alone.

---

## Public and Private Pathways

Where public writing, public critique, or inspectable discourse is encouraged, an equivalent rigorous private pathway must remain available where appropriate.

---

## Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course is highly compatible with evidence-linked discourse, revision-based learning, and structured critique of written work.

---

## Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror:
represent the concept, source, or system accurately

Water:
contextualize, organize, and interpret clearly

Fire:
revise, refine, and finalize a stronger written artifact

---

## Academic Integrity

Use of AI tools may require disclosure according to course instructions.
