# Course Catalog
## BS in Web3 Systems and Decentralized Infrastructure
## SydTek University

This catalog lists the major-core courses for the undergraduate degree.

---

## Major Core Courses

### WEB3-101 Introduction to Web3 Systems
Credits: 3

Introduces foundational concepts in Web3, including decentralization, blockchain, digital ownership, tokenized systems, and emerging governance models.

### WEB3-110 Internet Evolution: Web1, Web2, and Web3
Credits: 3

Examines the historical evolution of the internet from static publishing to platform centralization and decentralized infrastructure.

### WEB3-201 Blockchain Architecture and Distributed Systems
Credits: 3

Introduces distributed systems, blockchain architecture, consensus mechanisms, nodes, and network design.

### WEB3-220 Wallets, Identity, and Digital Ownership
Credits: 3

Explores wallets, cryptographic identity, digital custody, authorization, and ownership models in decentralized systems.

### WEB3-301 Smart Contracts and Decentralized Applications
Credits: 3

Examines smart contracts, programmable trust, decentralized applications, wallet interaction, and dApp architecture.

### WEB3-320 Tokenomics and Digital Economies
Credits: 3

Introduces digital incentive design, token supply, allocation, governance tokens, treasury systems, and sustainability models.

### WEB3-340 DAO Governance and Decentralized Organizations
Credits: 3

Studies decentralized autonomous organizations, governance structures, treasury stewardship, voting systems, and coordination design.

### WEB3-360 Web3 Security and Risk
Credits: 3

Examines security risks in decentralized systems, including smart contract vulnerabilities, governance risks, operational weaknesses, and trust failures.

### WEB3-410 Interoperability, Layer 2, and Emerging Infrastructure
Credits: 3

Explores scaling systems, Layer 2 architectures, interoperability, cross-chain design, modular systems, and emerging infrastructure.

### WEB3-490 Senior Project in Web3 Systems
Credits: 3

Capstone experience requiring students to synthesize architecture, governance, incentives, documentation, and professional presentation in a final project.

## Academic Evidence Note

In GitHub-based courses, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Public visibility may support authentic learning and portfolio development, but public exposure is not the academic standard by itself.
