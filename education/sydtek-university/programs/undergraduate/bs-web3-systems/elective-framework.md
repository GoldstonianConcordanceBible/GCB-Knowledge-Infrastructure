# Elective Framework
## BS in Web3 Systems and Decentralized Infrastructure
## SydTek University

This document defines the elective structure for the undergraduate degree.

---

## Purpose

Electives allow students to deepen, broaden, or specialize their learning beyond the core curriculum.

---

## Elective Categories

Students may select electives from areas such as:

- advanced Web3 applications
- AI and decentralized systems
- digital publishing and knowledge infrastructure
- entrepreneurship and innovation
- theology and philosophy of technology
- data analysis and visualization
- media, communication, and platform strategy

---

## Example Elective Titles

- WEB3-430 Governance Lab
- WEB3-420 Applied Web3 Studio
- AI-310 AI Agents and Knowledge Systems
- PUB-320 Machine-Readable Publishing
- ENT-310 Entrepreneurship in Emerging Technology
- PHIL-340 Philosophy of Technology
- DATA-330 Data Visualization for Digital Systems

---

## Approval Principle

Electives should support one or more of the following:

- degree coherence
- student specialization
- portfolio development
- professional readiness
- research preparation

---

## Evidence Standard

Where elective courses use GitHub-based evidence, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repositories may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.
