# BS in Fifth Gospel Studies

## Program Title
Bachelor of Science in Fifth Gospel Studies

## Academic Translation
Applied Theology and Religious Practice

## Program Purpose
The BS in Fifth Gospel Studies is an undergraduate theology degree designed to prepare students to study, embody, communicate, and apply Christian theology in contemporary life. The program is built on the conviction that theology is not only something to be learned intellectually but something to be lived visibly, ethically, and communicatively across generations, institutions, communities, and digital environments.

This degree serves as SydTek University’s flagship undergraduate theology program because it most clearly bridges biblical study, theological formation, public witness, ethical application, and modern communication. It is intentionally designed to preserve theological distinctiveness in public naming while remaining legible in standard higher education language for curriculum review, accreditation, assessment, and institutional governance.

## Program Rationale
The phrase Fifth Gospel is used here as a theological and formative concept. It communicates the idea that a person’s life functions as a visible testimony that others read, interpret, and remember. In academic terms, the degree focuses on applied theology, religious practice, discipleship formation, ethical reasoning, and theological communication.

The program is structured for students who want more than abstract doctrinal study. It is intended for students who want to understand Scripture, interpret theology responsibly, live with integrity, communicate belief clearly, and develop work that demonstrates theological understanding in action. This may include ministry, education, media, writing, research, digital communication, cultural analysis, nonprofit work, entrepreneurship, or community leadership.

## Program Description
The BS in Fifth Gospel Studies provides students with a structured course of study in biblical foundations, theological interpretation, Christian ethics, historical and systematic theology, applied discipleship, and theological communication. Students are trained to move from textual understanding to spiritual and ethical formation to practical witness in the world.

The degree combines core theological coursework with opportunities for pathway development, allowing students to build a focused area of application while remaining grounded in a coherent academic framework. Each student completes an integrative capstone demonstrating the ability to synthesize theology, ethics, communication, and lived practice.

## Institutional Role
This program functions as one of the primary undergraduate theology degrees within SydTek University’s theological portfolio. It is expected to serve multiple institutional roles:

- flagship public-facing undergraduate theology degree
- foundational program for students interested in ministry, teaching, writing, and digital faith communication
- entry point into individualized theological pathways
- model degree for future theology program development
- bridge between theological identity and accreditation-ready academic design

Because of its clarity, flexibility, and applicability, this degree is the strongest first candidate for full program development and institutional review.

## Degree Type
Bachelor of Science

The degree is designated as a Bachelor of Science because the program emphasizes structured learning outcomes, program assessment, research foundations, communication systems, ethical application, and integrative project-based work. While theological in content, it is designed with a systems-oriented academic structure that supports measurable competency development.

## Credit Hours
Total Degree Requirement: 120 semester credit hours

Recommended distribution:

General Education: 30 credits
Theology Core: 36 credits
Program Major Requirements: 36 credits
Pathway or Elective Credits: 12 credits
Capstone: 6 credits

This structure is designed to support standard undergraduate credit expectations while allowing sufficient room for depth in theology and individualized application.

## Program Distinctive
What makes the BS in Fifth Gospel Studies distinctive is that it treats theology as both academic study and embodied witness. The student is not only asked what they know, but what they can interpret, communicate, build, and live.

The degree is therefore organized around five integrated dimensions:

biblical understanding  
theological formation  
ethical application  
cultural communication  
integrative witness  

These dimensions shape the curriculum, the learning outcomes, and the capstone expectation.

## Student Profile
This program is designed for students who may see themselves in one or more of the following roles:

- future ministry leaders
- Christian educators
- writers and authors
- digital communicators
- researchers in theology and culture
- community builders
- nonprofit and mission-oriented leaders
- students seeking a theology-centered degree with modern application

The degree is also suitable for students who want a strong undergraduate theological formation before entering graduate theological study, seminary, ministry training, education, law, public service, or entrepreneurial work.

## Program Goals
The BS in Fifth Gospel Studies is intended to produce graduates who can:

understand core biblical and theological concepts  
interpret Scripture responsibly  
connect theology to ethical and cultural issues  
communicate theological ideas clearly across media  
demonstrate Christian formation through integrative work  
complete a coherent course of study with documented academic rigor  

These goals guide curriculum development and provide the foundation for measurable program learning outcomes.

## Program Learning Outcomes
Graduates of the BS in Fifth Gospel Studies will be able to:

1. Interpret biblical texts using established hermeneutical methods and theological reasoning.
2. Explain major themes in biblical, historical, systematic, and applied theology.
3. Apply Christian theological principles to contemporary ethical, cultural, and social questions.
4. Communicate theological ideas effectively in written, oral, and digital formats.
5. Produce original integrative work that demonstrates the relationship between theology, practice, and public witness.
6. Demonstrate academic and spiritual maturity through sustained reflection, disciplined inquiry, and capstone-level synthesis.

## Curriculum Philosophy
The curriculum is built on progression rather than randomness.

Students begin with biblical and theological foundations.
They then move into interpretation, ethics, doctrine, and historical understanding.
From there they develop applied skill in communication, leadership, research, and contextual engagement.
Finally, they complete pathway work and a capstone that demonstrates integrative learning.

This progression ensures that the degree remains coherent and defensible in accreditation review. Students are not simply collecting interesting religion courses. They are moving through a designed formation-and-learning sequence.

## Pathway Flexibility
The program supports structured flexibility through approved pathways. Students may select a concentration of emphasis or propose a supervised individualized path, provided that all coursework remains aligned to program learning outcomes and credit-hour expectations.

Potential pathways may include:

- theology and culture
- theology and media
- theology and education
- theology and leadership
- theology and digital ministry
- individualized applied theology pathway

No custom pathway is considered valid without faculty review, documented rationale, and outcome alignment. This preserves both flexibility and academic integrity.

## Delivery Format
The program may be offered in on-campus, online, hybrid, and AI-assisted instructional formats, subject to institutional policy and faculty oversight.

All delivery modes must preserve equivalent academic expectations, assessment standards, learning outcomes, and student support structures.

## Faculty Oversight
The program is governed by qualified faculty responsible for curriculum approval, course review, pathway oversight, assessment interpretation, and continuous improvement. Faculty teaching in the program should possess credentials appropriate to accreditation expectations in theology, religious studies, ministry, ethics, or related fields.

Faculty oversight is required in the following areas:

- course design and revision
- approval of individualized pathways
- assessment review
- capstone supervision
- annual program evaluation

## Assessment Philosophy
Assessment in the BS in Fifth Gospel Studies is based on direct and indirect evidence of student learning.

Direct measures may include:
- exegetical papers
- theology essays
- oral presentations
- digital communication projects
- capstone artifacts
- rubric-scored signature assignments

Indirect measures may include:
- student surveys
- graduate feedback
- pathway review data
- course evaluations
- retention and completion data

Assessment results are reviewed annually and used for program improvement.

## Capstone Expectation
All students must complete a culminating capstone experience that demonstrates integrative learning across the degree. The capstone must show that the student can synthesize biblical study, theology, ethics, communication, and practical application into one substantial project.

Possible capstone formats may include:
- theological manuscript or book project
- teaching series
- research thesis
- media or communication portfolio
- ministry or community application project
- structured digital theology resource

Every capstone must be faculty-approved and assessed using a standardized rubric aligned to program learning outcomes.

## Accreditation Readiness Position
The BS in Fifth Gospel Studies is designed to meet the expectations of regional accreditation review by demonstrating the following:

- clear program purpose
- defined student learning outcomes
- standard undergraduate credit structure
- curricular coherence
- faculty governance
- assessment and improvement processes
- pathway controls
- capstone evidence of learning

The program’s theological naming does not weaken accreditation readiness because it is supported by clear academic translation, formal curriculum architecture, and documented assessment structures.

## Strategic Importance to SydTek
This degree is strategically important because it gives SydTek University a strong theological flagship at the undergraduate level. It establishes a program that is distinctive in branding, clear in purpose, scalable in structure, and usable as a model for future theology program development.

It is not merely a degree title.
It is a foundational academic vehicle for building the university’s broader theological identity in a way that remains understandable to both public audiences and formal academic reviewers.
