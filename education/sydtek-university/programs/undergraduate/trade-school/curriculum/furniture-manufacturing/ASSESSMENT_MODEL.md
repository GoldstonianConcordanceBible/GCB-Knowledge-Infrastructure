# Assessment Model
## Furniture Manufacturing Curriculum

The Furniture Manufacturing curriculum uses a portfolio-based and performance-based assessment model.

Traditional written exams may support instruction, but the primary evidence of learning is the student’s ability to build, document, and refine real furniture work.

---

## Assessment Categories

### 1. Safety Performance
Students are assessed on:

- workshop conduct
- PPE compliance
- safe machine behavior
- cleanup discipline
- adherence to lab rules

### 2. Tool Competency
Students are assessed on:

- correct tool identification
- proper setup procedures
- handling control
- maintenance awareness
- accuracy of use

### 3. Fabrication Accuracy
Students are assessed on:

- measurements
- squareness
- fitment
- alignment
- structural integrity

### 4. Surface Quality
Students are assessed on:

- sanding consistency
- defect reduction
- finishing preparation
- stain or seal application quality

### 5. Documentation Quality
Students are assessed on:

- build logs
- cut lists
- materials lists
- process notes
- photographic evidence

### 6. Capstone Readiness
Students are assessed on:

- completion
- function
- durability
- presentation
- commercial usability

---

## Evidence Types

Accepted evidence includes:

- completed practice pieces
- workshop observations
- instructor sign-offs
- documented correction cycles
- GitHub project files
- capstone product presentation

---

## Grading Logic

The program prioritizes demonstrated competence.

Suggested grading distribution:

- 25% safety and workshop discipline
- 20% tool competency
- 25% fabrication quality
- 10% finishing quality
- 10% documentation quality
- 10% capstone presentation
