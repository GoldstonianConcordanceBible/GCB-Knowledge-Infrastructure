# Term Sequence
## Furniture Manufacturing Curriculum

---

## Term 1 — Foundations of Woodcraft

Term 1 establishes workshop discipline and technical accuracy.

Students begin by learning how to operate safely, identify core materials, measure correctly, and use foundational tools. This term prevents weak habits from forming early in the program.

Primary outcomes:

- safe behavior in the workshop
- familiarity with tools and materials
- measurement accuracy
- introductory cutting and shaping control

---

## Term 2 — Structural Furniture Construction

Term 2 transitions students from isolated exercises into complete furniture systems.

Students begin producing furniture components and assembling structurally sound builds. This term emphasizes strength, symmetry, weight distribution, and joinery integrity.

Primary outcomes:

- competent joinery
- structural assembly
- table construction
- chair and rocking chair systems

---

## Term 3 — Production and Finishing Systems

Term 3 introduces repeatability and refinement.

Students learn how to move from one-off construction into efficient production logic. The focus shifts toward surface quality, workflow organization, and commercial consistency.

Primary outcomes:

- workflow efficiency
- repeatable production systems
- finishing quality
- repair competency
- strong documentation habits

---

## Term 4 — Commercial Capstone

Term 4 culminates in a finished product that can be presented commercially.

Students select a product, execute a complete build, document the process, and prepare the final item for sale or professional presentation.

Primary outcomes:

- commercial-grade capstone build
- final portfolio proof
- product presentation
- market-facing documentation
