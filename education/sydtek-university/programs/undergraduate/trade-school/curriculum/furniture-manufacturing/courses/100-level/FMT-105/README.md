# FMT 105
## Power Tools and Machine Operation

Course Code: FMT 105  
Course Level: 100  
Term Placement: Term 1 — Foundations of Woodcraft

---

## Course Description

FMT 105 introduces students to the safe and controlled use of foundational woodworking power tools and shop machines.

Students learn the purpose, setup logic, risk zones, and operating procedures for tools commonly used in furniture manufacturing, including drills, sanders, routers, miter saws, table saws, planers, jointers, and bandsaws where available.

The course emphasizes that machine use is a privilege earned through safety discipline and attention to procedure.

---

## Course Purpose

The purpose of this course is to prepare students for supervised machine use in furniture construction labs.

Students learn that machine accuracy depends on setup, attention, and discipline rather than speed alone.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- identify major power tools used in the furniture shop
- explain the purpose of each machine
- recognize machine hazard zones
- perform pre-operation checks
- demonstrate safe setup habits
- perform basic cuts and shaping operations under supervision
- maintain clean and safe machine areas
- shut down and reset machines correctly after use

---

## Lab Requirements

Students are required to participate in:

- machine identification walkthroughs
- hazard zone mapping
- pre-operation checklist drills
- supervised tool startup procedures
- guided cutting demonstrations
- sanding and shaping practice
- machine cleanup and shutdown drills

---

## Assessment Criteria

Students are assessed on:

- safety readiness
- machine identification
- understanding of operating sequence
- respect for exclusion zones
- ability to follow supervised procedures
- cleanup and reset consistency

---

## Required Deliverables

- machine identification worksheet
- power tool safety checklist
- supervised operation log
- hazard zone exercise
- machine cleanup sign-off
