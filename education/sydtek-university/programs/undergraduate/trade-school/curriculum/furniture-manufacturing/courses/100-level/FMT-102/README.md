# FMT 102
## Lumber Science and Material Selection

Course Code: FMT 102  
Course Level: 100  
Term Placement: Term 1 — Foundations of Woodcraft

---

## Course Description

FMT 102 introduces students to the characteristics of wood as a living material used in furniture fabrication.

Students study wood species, grain patterns, moisture movement, board quality, defects, stability, and selection logic for different furniture applications.

This course helps students understand why material knowledge is essential to durable and attractive furniture.

---

## Course Purpose

The purpose of this course is to teach students how to choose the right wood for the right project and to understand how wood behavior affects fabrication outcomes.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- distinguish hardwood and softwood categories
- identify common lumber species used in furniture work
- describe grain direction and wood movement
- recognize basic defects in lumber
- evaluate board quality for a given build
- explain moisture-related risks in furniture construction

---

## Lab Requirements

Students are required to participate in:

- board identification exercises
- grain analysis drills
- defect recognition practice
- moisture and movement discussion labs
- lumber selection walkthroughs

---

## Assessment Criteria

Students are assessed on:

- species identification accuracy
- understanding of wood behavior
- material selection logic
- ability to detect visible defects
- participation in shop material reviews

---

## Required Deliverables

- lumber identification worksheet
- grain direction exercise
- defect classification sheet
- material selection reflection
