# Term 1 Completion
## Foundations of Woodcraft

Term 1 establishes the workshop habits and baseline competencies required for advancement into structural furniture construction.

Students are not advanced based on time alone. Advancement depends on demonstrated readiness.

---

## Courses Included

- FMT 101 — Workshop Safety and Shop Discipline
- FMT 102 — Lumber Science and Material Selection
- FMT 103 — Measuring, Layout, and Dimensional Accuracy
- FMT 104 — Hand Tools and Manual Woodcraft
- FMT 105 — Power Tools and Machine Operation

---

## Required Competencies for Advancement

Students must demonstrate:

- safe conduct in a workshop environment
- PPE compliance
- understanding of wood behavior
- accurate measuring and layout
- controlled use of core hand tools
- safe readiness for supervised machine use
- ability to maintain a clean work area
- basic documentation habits

---

## Required Completion Evidence

Students must submit or complete the following:

- safety acknowledgment and conduct sign-off
- lumber identification exercises
- layout and measuring exercises
- hand tool practice boards
- machine safety and operation checklists
- instructor readiness review

---

## Advancement Decision

Students who complete Term 1 successfully may advance into the 200-level structural furniture sequence.

Students who do not yet demonstrate readiness may be assigned corrective practice before progression.

---

## Instructional Rationale

The first term protects the rest of the program.

Weak habits in safety, measuring, and tool handling become larger problems in structural furniture work. For that reason, Term 1 is treated as the gatekeeping phase for all later fabrication labs.
