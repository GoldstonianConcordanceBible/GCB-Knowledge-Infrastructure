# FMT 103
## Measuring, Layout, and Dimensional Accuracy

Course Code: FMT 103  
Course Level: 100  
Term Placement: Term 1 — Foundations of Woodcraft

---

## Course Description

FMT 103 trains students in the measurement systems and layout logic required for precise woodworking.

Students learn how to read measuring tools, mark materials accurately, establish reference lines, verify squareness, and prepare wood for clean cutting and assembly.

This course is essential because poor measurement habits lead directly to fabrication errors.

---

## Course Purpose

The purpose of this course is to train students to work with accuracy, consistency, and dimensional discipline before they move into structural furniture construction.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- read a tape measure accurately
- use layout tools correctly
- mark dimensions clearly on lumber
- establish reference faces and edges
- verify squareness
- reduce cumulative measurement error
- prepare accurate cut layouts

---

## Lab Requirements

Students are required to participate in:

- measuring drills
- line marking exercises
- square verification practice
- cut layout planning
- dimension transfer exercises

---

## Assessment Criteria

Students are assessed on:

- measurement accuracy
- clarity of marks
- layout consistency
- ability to maintain dimensional control
- error detection and correction

---

## Required Deliverables

- tape measure competency sheet
- layout practice board
- square verification exercise
- dimensional accuracy check
- cut planning worksheet
