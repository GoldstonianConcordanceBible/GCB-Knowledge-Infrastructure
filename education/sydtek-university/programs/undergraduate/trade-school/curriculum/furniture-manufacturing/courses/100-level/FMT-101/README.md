# FMT 101
## Workshop Safety and Shop Discipline

Course Code: FMT 101  
Course Level: 100  
Term Placement: Term 1 — Foundations of Woodcraft

---

## Course Description

FMT 101 introduces students to the safety systems, behavioral expectations, and daily operating procedures of the furniture workshop.

Students learn the core principles of safe conduct, personal protective equipment, machine awareness, shop organization, hazard identification, and cleanup discipline.

This course establishes the behavioral foundation for all later fabrication work.

---

## Course Purpose

The purpose of this course is to build a safety-first mindset in students before they begin active furniture production.

Students are expected to understand that quality work begins with disciplined workshop behavior.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- identify major workshop hazards
- explain the function of required PPE
- demonstrate safe movement in a shared workshop
- perform beginning and end-of-day shop procedures
- follow instructor-directed machine safety rules
- maintain a clean and organized work area
- respond appropriately to common workshop incidents

---

## Lab Requirements

Students are required to participate in:

- safety orientation walkthrough
- PPE inspection exercises
- shop zoning and hazard mapping
- cleanup and reset drills
- machine exclusion zone practice
- emergency response briefing

---

## Assessment Criteria

Students are assessed on:

- safety compliance
- attentiveness to workshop rules
- PPE readiness
- cleanup consistency
- hazard recognition
- professional conduct in the lab

---

## Required Deliverables

- signed safety acknowledgment
- PPE checklist
- hazard identification worksheet
- workshop conduct evaluation
- lab cleanup sign-off

---

## Progression Function

FMT 101 must be completed before students are cleared for tool-intensive fabrication work.
