# FMT 201
## Joinery Systems for Furniture

Course Code: FMT 201  
Course Level: 200  
Term Placement: Term 2 — Structural Furniture Construction

---

## Course Description

FMT 201 introduces students to the structural joinery systems that hold furniture together.

Students learn how different joints distribute weight, resist movement, and maintain alignment under long-term stress.

The course explores both traditional woodworking joints and modern furniture joinery techniques.

---

## Course Purpose

The purpose of this course is to train students to create strong, precise connections between furniture components.

Joinery is the backbone of furniture manufacturing, and mastery of joints determines the longevity and strength of a finished piece.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- identify common furniture joinery systems
- explain structural advantages of each joint type
- cut simple joinery by hand
- prepare joinery using power tools
- assemble joints with correct alignment
- evaluate joint strength and accuracy
- repair poorly cut joints

---

## Joinery Systems Introduced

Students study and practice:

- butt joints
- lap joints
- dowel joints
- mortise and tenon
- pocket screw systems
- reinforced corner joints

---

## Lab Requirements

Students must complete:

- joinery sample boards
- mortise and tenon practice pieces
- dowel alignment exercise
- reinforced corner construction
- instructor critique of joinery accuracy

---

## Assessment Criteria

Students are assessed on:

- joint alignment
- tightness of fit
- structural stability
- repeatability of cuts
- correction of mistakes

---

## Required Deliverables

- joinery practice board
- mortise and tenon sample
- dowel alignment project
- structural corner assembly
