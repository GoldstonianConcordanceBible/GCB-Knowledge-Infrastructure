# GAME 145 – Introduction to Game Design and Intelligent Systems

## Course Code
GAME 145

## Credits
2

## Delivery Format
On-campus, online, hybrid, and MOOC-supported

## Prerequisites
None

## Course Description
This course introduces students to foundational elements of game design, including mechanics, systems thinking, player experience, prototyping, and design iteration. Students examine a range of digital games to learn how structure, rules, balance, and player motivation shape the design process. The course also introduces intelligent systems in games, including non-player behavior, procedural design concepts, and the growing role of AI in game development, testing, and interactive design workflows.

## Course Learning Outcomes
1. Explain foundational principles of game design, systems thinking, and player experience.
2. Analyze how mechanics, rules, and structure shape gameplay and engagement.
3. Evaluate how intelligent systems and AI concepts appear in games and game development workflows.
4. Develop a basic game concept or prototype informed by design principles and iterative feedback.

Students are introduced to AI in games both as a design concept and as a workflow-support tool. The course emphasizes critical understanding rather than blind tool use.

- AI-assisted design workflows and testing support

### Week 7
- Game concept workshop

- Final concept or prototype presentation
- Synthesis and reflection
