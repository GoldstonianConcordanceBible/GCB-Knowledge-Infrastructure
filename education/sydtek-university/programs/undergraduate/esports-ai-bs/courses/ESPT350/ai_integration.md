# ESPT 350 AI Integration

## AI Role in the Course
AI appears in this course primarily as:
- a system to be critiqued
- a comparative reasoning tool
- a source of claims about human performance that require skepticism and review

Students examine how AI may be used to:
- summarize behavioral trends
- infer player tendencies
- visualize performance patterns
- support coaching or reflective processes

## Required AI Learning Activity
Students complete an AI behavior modeling critique in which they:
- examine an AI-supported performance claim or output
- identify what seems useful, weak, reductive, or ethically problematic
- compare AI framing to course-based psychological reasoning
- explain where human interpretation remains essential

## Ethical Focus
Students should address:
- reduction of human complexity into oversimplified metrics
- privacy and surveillance concerns
- questionable behavioral inference
- misuse of dashboards as authority
- risks of false objectivity

## Disclosure
Any approved AI-assisted work must be disclosed according to course and program policy.

## Evidence of AI Learning
The AI behavior modeling critique is the principal AI-learning artifact in this course.
