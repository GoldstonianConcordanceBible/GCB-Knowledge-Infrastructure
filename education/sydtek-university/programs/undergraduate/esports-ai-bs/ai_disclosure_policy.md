# AI Disclosure Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines student and faculty expectations for the disclosure of approved artificial intelligence use in academic work.

## Policy Statement
Artificial intelligence may be used in approved ways to support learning, research, planning, analysis, and production. When AI use is permitted or required, such use must be disclosed according to course and program expectations.

## Why Disclosure Matters
Disclosure supports:
- academic honesty
- transparency of process
- accurate assessment of student learning
- ethical use of AI tools
- responsible authorship and revision practices

## Student Responsibilities
Students must:
- follow course-specific AI use instructions
- disclose approved AI use when required
- identify the purpose of AI use
- evaluate AI output critically
- verify claims, data, and sources
- retain responsibility for the final submission

## Faculty Responsibilities
Faculty should:
- state whether AI use is required, optional, limited, or prohibited for each assignment
- provide clear disclosure expectations
- evaluate student learning rather than undisclosed automation
- preserve AI-related evidence when needed for assessment

## Minimum Disclosure Elements
When disclosure is required, students should document:
- tool used
- purpose of use
- general prompt or task description
- summary of output used
- explanation of revision, verification, and final ownership

## Non-Disclosure
Failure to disclose required AI use may be treated as an academic integrity concern under institutional policy.
