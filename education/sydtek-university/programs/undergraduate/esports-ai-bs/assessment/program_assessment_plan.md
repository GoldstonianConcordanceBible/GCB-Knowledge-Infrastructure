# Program Assessment Plan

## Purpose
The purpose of this assessment plan is to measure student learning, program effectiveness, AI integration compliance, and continuous improvement within the Bachelor of Science in Esports, Gaming, and AI Performance.

## Assessment Philosophy
The program uses an outcomes-based assessment model that combines direct and indirect evidence of student learning. Assessment is used not only for compliance, but also for instructional improvement, curriculum revision, and program growth.

## Assessment Cycle
- Course-level assessment: each term
- Program-level review: annually
- Curriculum review: annually
- Major revision review: as needed based on evidence, labor-market shifts, technology changes, or institutional priorities

## Sources of Evidence
### Direct Evidence
- Major assignments
- Projects
- Presentations
- Exams and quizzes
- Capstone artifacts
- Broadcast/event production artifacts
- Analytics dashboards
- AI disclosure logs

### Indirect Evidence
- Student surveys
- Faculty reflections
- Course evaluations
- Internship or field feedback when applicable
- Advisory feedback
- Retention and completion trends

## Program Learning Outcomes Assessed
- PLO 1: Esports ecosystem analysis
- PLO 2: AI application in workflows and strategy
- PLO 3: Digital marketing and fan engagement strategy
- PLO 4: Wellness, performance, and human-centered assessment
- PLO 5: Media, streaming, and live production
- PLO 6: Revenue, partnerships, and entrepreneurship
- PLO 7: Leadership, administration, and ethics
- PLO 8: Data interpretation and decision support

## Performance Benchmarks
Unless otherwise revised by faculty governance, the following benchmarks apply:
- 70 percent of students will meet or exceed proficiency on each assessed PLO
- 80 percent of courses will submit complete assessment artifacts on schedule
- 85 percent of students will submit required AI usage disclosures in courses requiring AI-supported work
- Program completion, persistence, and engagement data will be reviewed annually for improvement opportunities

## Annual Review Questions
- Which outcomes are strongest?
- Which outcomes need reinforcement?
- Which courses show gaps in performance?
- Is AI integration improving student readiness or creating confusion?
- Are assessment tools aligned with course and program outcomes?
- What curriculum changes are justified by the evidence?

## Reporting
Results are documented in annual review files, faculty review summaries, and evidence logs stored in the assessment folder.
