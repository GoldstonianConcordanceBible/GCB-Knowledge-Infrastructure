# Student Complaint Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the process by which students may submit and seek resolution for academic, administrative, or program-related complaints.

## Policy Statement
SydTek University is committed to fair, timely, respectful, and documented review of student complaints.

## Scope
This policy may apply to concerns involving:
- course delivery
- faculty interaction
- grading process concerns not otherwise governed by formal grade appeal procedure
- program administration
- access to learning resources
- student support services
- AI usage expectations or disclosure misunderstandings
- communication failures affecting academic participation

## Informal Resolution
Students are encouraged, when appropriate, to first seek informal resolution by addressing the issue with the relevant instructor, staff member, or program lead.

## Formal Complaint Submission
If informal resolution is unsuccessful or inappropriate, the student may submit a formal complaint that includes:
- student name and contact information
- date of submission
- description of the issue
- relevant course or program context
- steps already taken to resolve the matter
- requested resolution

## Review Process
1. Complaint received and logged
2. Initial acknowledgment sent
3. Review assigned to appropriate academic or administrative authority
4. Relevant documentation gathered
5. Decision or response issued
6. Record retained according to policy

## Non-Retaliation
Students submitting complaints in good faith should not be subject to retaliation.

## Documentation
Complaint records should be maintained in a secure and reviewable manner consistent with institutional practice.
