AIS-240 Course Shell
AI Research Methods

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Introduces research methods for artificial intelligence including problem definition, literature review, experiment design, baseline comparison, reproducibility, reporting standards, and evidence-linked technical writing.

Students learn how to frame AI questions, assess sources, document methodology, and communicate results in academically inspectable formats.

Prerequisites

AIS-201

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 define research questions relevant to AI systems and applications
2 conduct literature review and source-based technical synthesis
3 design basic experiments with baseline and comparison logic
4 document methods, limitations, and findings clearly
5 produce reproducible, inspectable research artifacts using GitHub-based workflows

GitHub Evidence Statement

Where GitHub-based evidence is used, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may include inspectable peer review, critique of research claims, and evidence-linked revision practices.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent sources, methods, and claims accurately  
Water: interpret results, limitations, and comparative evidence  
Fire: refine and publish reproducible AI research artifacts
