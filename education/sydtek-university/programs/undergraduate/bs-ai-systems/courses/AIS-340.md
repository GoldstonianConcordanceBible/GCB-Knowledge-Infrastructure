AIS-340 Course Shell
AI Ethics and Governance

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Examines ethical, legal, social, and governance issues in artificial intelligence including bias, fairness, transparency, accountability, privacy, labor impacts, and institutional responsibility.

Students evaluate how AI systems affect individuals, communities, and organizations and how governance frameworks guide responsible design and deployment.

Prerequisites

AIS-201

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 explain major ethical and governance issues in artificial intelligence
2 analyze bias, fairness, transparency, and accountability concerns in AI systems
3 evaluate the social and institutional implications of AI deployment
4 compare governance frameworks and responsible AI practices
5 document evidence-based ethical analysis through inspectable academic artifacts

GitHub Evidence Statement

Where GitHub-based evidence is used, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may use structured critique of real-world AI controversies, policy arguments, and governance claims.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent ethical issues, stakeholders, and governance concepts accurately  
Water: interpret impacts, tradeoffs, and contested claims responsibly  
Fire: produce documented, evidence-linked ethical analysis and governance recommendations
