AIS-101 Course Shell
Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Introduces the foundations of artificial intelligence including symbolic reasoning, machine learning, neural networks, search, and intelligent agents.

Students examine the historical development of AI and major approaches to building systems that simulate perception, prediction, reasoning, and decision-making.

Prerequisites

None

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 define artificial intelligence and distinguish major historical approaches
2 explain foundational concepts including search, reasoning, learning, and agents
3 compare symbolic AI and machine learning methods
4 analyze basic AI workflows and use cases
5 document course work through inspectable academic artifacts

GitHub Evidence Statement

Where GitHub-based evidence is used, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may use structured critique of AI systems, historical developments, and public claims about artificial intelligence.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent AI concepts, systems, and terminology accurately  
Water: interpret approaches, limitations, and comparative strengths  
Fire: synthesize course learning into a documented AI analysis artifact
