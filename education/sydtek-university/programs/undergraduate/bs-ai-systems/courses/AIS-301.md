AIS-301 Course Shell
Deep Learning Systems

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Introduces deep learning systems with emphasis on multilayer architectures, representation learning, optimization, regularization, training workflows, and performance analysis.

Students examine how deeper model structures support vision, language, and prediction tasks while also studying the computational and design tradeoffs of deep learning.

Prerequisites

AIS-220

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 explain the principles of deep learning and representation learning
2 compare deep architectures used for major AI tasks
3 train and evaluate deep learning models using reproducible workflows
4 analyze optimization, regularization, and performance tradeoffs
5 document system design, experiments, and findings through inspectable artifacts

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may use structured critique of benchmark claims, training decisions, model limitations, and computational tradeoffs.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent deep learning architectures, training processes, and evaluation methods accurately  
Water: interpret model behavior, optimization outcomes, and system tradeoffs  
Fire: train, refine, and document deep learning systems through inspectable academic workflows
