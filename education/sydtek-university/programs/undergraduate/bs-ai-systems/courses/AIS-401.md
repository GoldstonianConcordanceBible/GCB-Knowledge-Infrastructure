AIS-401 Course Shell
Advanced AI Architectures

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Examines advanced architectures in artificial intelligence including transformer systems, multimodal models, retrieval-augmented pipelines, foundation models, and emerging design patterns in large-scale AI.

Students analyze how advanced architectures extend earlier machine learning and deep learning concepts into scalable, production-relevant systems.

Prerequisites

AIS-301  
AIS-230

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 explain the structure and purpose of advanced AI architectures
2 compare transformer, multimodal, and retrieval-augmented system designs
3 analyze tradeoffs among scale, accuracy, efficiency, and interpretability
4 evaluate how advanced architectures support language, vision, and hybrid tasks
5 document architectural analysis and experimental findings through inspectable academic artifacts

GitHub Evidence Statement

Where GitHub-based evidence is used, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may use structured critique of model scaling claims, foundation model limitations, and architectural tradeoffs in current AI systems.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent advanced architectures, components, and workflows accurately  
Water: interpret system tradeoffs, capabilities, and limitations responsibly  
Fire: synthesize, evaluate, and document advanced AI architecture analysis
