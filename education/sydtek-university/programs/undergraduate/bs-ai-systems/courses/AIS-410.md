AIS-410 Course Shell
AI for Web3 Systems

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Studies the integration of artificial intelligence with Web3 systems including on-chain analytics, autonomous agents, governance support tools, tokenized ecosystems, decentralized data environments, and intelligent interfaces for blockchain-based applications.

Students examine how AI can support decentralized infrastructure, decision systems, and agent-based workflows in Web3 contexts.

Prerequisites

AIS-310  
WEB3-201 or equivalent program approval

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 explain key integration points between AI and Web3 systems
2 analyze how intelligent agents and models can support decentralized applications
3 evaluate AI use cases in governance, analytics, and autonomous coordination
4 document architectural and operational tradeoffs in AI-Web3 environments
5 produce inspectable academic artifacts demonstrating AI-Web3 system design

GitHub Evidence Statement

Where GitHub-based evidence is used, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may include structured critique of decentralized AI claims, agent governance, token incentives, and system trust assumptions.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent AI-Web3 architectures, protocols, and workflows accurately  
Water: interpret tradeoffs in governance, autonomy, and decentralized coordination  
Fire: design and document AI-enabled Web3 systems through inspectable artifacts
