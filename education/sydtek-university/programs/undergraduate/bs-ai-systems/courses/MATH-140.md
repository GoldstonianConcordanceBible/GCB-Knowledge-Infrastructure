MATH-140 Course Shell
Probability and Statistics for AI

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Introduces probability and statistics as foundations for artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data analysis.

Students study distributions, conditional probability, inference, estimation, hypothesis testing, and uncertainty in computational decision-making.

Prerequisites

None

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 explain major concepts in probability and statistics relevant to AI
2 apply distributions, likelihood, and inference to basic problems
3 interpret uncertainty and variation in data-driven systems
4 analyze how statistical reasoning supports machine learning workflows
5 document quantitative findings in inspectable academic formats

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Water: interpret uncertainty, inference, and analytical results  
Fire: apply and document statistical reasoning in AI-related tasks
