AIS-330 Course Shell
AI Knowledge Graphs

Credits: 3  
Level: Undergraduate

Catalog Description

Introduces knowledge graphs, ontologies, semantic structure, entity relationships, metadata design, and reasoning-oriented information systems for artificial intelligence.

Students examine how structured knowledge layers support retrieval, interpretation, and AI-native infrastructure.

Prerequisites

AIS-210  
AIS-230

Course Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1 explain the role of knowledge graphs and ontologies in AI systems
2 design basic graph structures for entities, relationships, and metadata
3 compare structured knowledge approaches with unstructured data workflows
4 analyze how graph-based systems support reasoning and retrieval
5 document schema, relationship logic, and graph artifacts through inspectable repositories

GitHub Evidence Statement

Where GitHub-based evidence is used, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Open Discussion Pedagogy Note

This course may include structured critique of ontology design, metadata quality, and knowledge representation tradeoffs.

Mirror → Water → Fire Alignment

Mirror: represent entities, relationships, and semantic structure accurately  
Water: interpret connections, classifications, and graph-based meaning  
Fire: build and document knowledge graph artifacts for AI use cases
