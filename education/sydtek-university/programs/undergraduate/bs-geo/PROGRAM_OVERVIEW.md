# Program Overview

## Program Purpose
The purpose of the BS-GEO is to prepare students to understand and shape the next layer of digital visibility: the layer where AI systems retrieve, synthesize, and prioritize information. Students learn to design authoritative, structured, provenance-rich systems capable of being recalled by models rather than merely indexed by search engines.

## Program Distinctive
This degree is distinguished by five commitments:

1. **AI-native design**  
   The program is built for generative systems from the outset.

2. **Knowledge graph centrality**  
   Students learn entities, relationships, schemas, and ontological structure as core professional skills.

3. **Machine-readable publication**  
   Every major deliverable is designed for agent and LLM interpretation.

4. **Provenance and authority**  
   Students are trained to anchor outputs in verifiable, citable, timestamped systems.

5. **Public evidence architecture**  
   Learning is documented in repositories, structured documents, and media ecosystems that create durable evidence of competence.

## Program Philosophy
The BS-GEO treats information architecture as both technical and moral work. Students are trained not simply to optimize visibility, but to optimize truthful, well-structured, responsibly governed knowledge. The SydTek doctrinal sequence of Mirror, Water, and Fire provides the philosophical framework for this progression.

## Delivery Model
The degree uses a hybrid artifact-driven model including:

- repository-based instruction
- recorded lectures and video modules
- structured reflections
- discussion and engagement artifacts
- schema and dataset submissions
- cumulative public and semi-public portfolio evidence

## Graduate Profile
A graduate of the BS-GEO will be able to:
- define GEO as a technical and strategic discipline
- design ontology-backed knowledge systems
- produce machine-readable publications
- architect AI-visible authority layers
- deploy GitHub-centered evidence systems
- connect structured content to scholarly and public distribution channels
