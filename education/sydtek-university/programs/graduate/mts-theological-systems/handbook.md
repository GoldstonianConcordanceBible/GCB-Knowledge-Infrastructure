# MTS Program Handbook  
Master of Theological Systems (MTS)  
SydTek University  

---

## 1. Purpose of the Handbook

This handbook defines the academic, intellectual, and operational standards for students enrolled in the Master of Theological Systems (MTS) program.

It establishes expectations for:
- Graduate-level thinking  
- Research rigor  
- Theological system-building  
- AI-native knowledge production  

---

## 2. Academic Philosophy

The MTS program is built on the Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine:

- Mirror: Faithful representation of scripture and source material  
- Water: Interpretation, comparison, and synthesis  
- Fire: Application, publication, and dissemination  

All student work must demonstrate progression through these three stages.

---

## 3. Credit Structure

Total Credits: 30–36 Graduate Credits  

Typical Breakdown:
- Core Courses: 24–27 credits  
- Thesis / Capstone: 6–9 credits  

Each course is structured around:
- Playlist-based content engagement  
- Written and structured outputs  
- Research-driven assignments  

---

## 4. Learning Model

The program uses a hybrid academic model:

- Content Layer: Shorts and playlists (input)  
- Course Layer: Structured modules and analysis  
- Research Layer: Formal writing and system-building  
- Publication Layer: Output for academic and public use  

Students are expected to actively move content from one layer to the next.

---

## 5. Academic Expectations

Graduate students must demonstrate:

- Independent thinking  
- Theological depth  
- Cross-textual analysis  
- System-level reasoning  
- Clarity in written and structured outputs  

Passive consumption of content is not sufficient for success in this program.

---

## 6. Research Standards

All research must:

- Be grounded in scripture and/or historical sources  
- Demonstrate clear argument structure  
- Engage with multiple perspectives when applicable  
- Maintain internal logical consistency  
- Be documented in a structured, reviewable format  

Students are encouraged to integrate:
- Canonical texts  
- Apocryphal works  
- Historical sources  
- Philosophical frameworks  

---

## 7. Writing and Output Requirements

Students must produce:

- Research papers  
- Comparative analyses  
- Theological frameworks  
- Structured outlines and models  
- Optional ontology and metadata artifacts  

All outputs should be:
- Clear  
- Organized  
- Reusable  
- Publishable  

---

## 8. AI and GEO Expectations

The MTS program is AI-native.

Students are expected to:

- Structure their work for discoverability  
- Use consistent terminology  
- Tag and organize concepts  
- Build outputs that can be indexed by AI systems  

This includes:
- Metadata tagging  
- Structured outlines  
- Semantic clarity  

---

## 9. GitHub and Repository Use

All students must maintain a structured repository for their work.

Repositories should include:

- Course outputs  
- Research drafts  
- Final papers  
- Metadata files  
- Supporting materials  

Repositories function as:
- Academic portfolios  
- Evidence of work  
- Foundations for publication  

---

## 10. Assessment Philosophy

Assessment is based on demonstrated capability.

Evaluation criteria include:

- Depth of analysis  
- Clarity of thought  
- Quality of argument  
- Structural organization  
- Originality  
- Application of doctrine  

Grades, where used, reflect the quality of produced work, not time spent.

---

## 11. Academic Integrity

Students must:

- Produce original work  
- Accurately represent sources  
- Avoid misrepresentation of ideas  
- Maintain honesty in all submissions  

Use of AI is permitted, but:

- Students must understand and own their outputs  
- Work must reflect genuine comprehension  

---

## 12. Thesis Requirement

All students must complete a Master’s Thesis.

The thesis must:

- Address a defined research problem  
- Integrate multiple course areas  
- Demonstrate advanced theological reasoning  
- Be suitable for publication  

Optional:
- Convert thesis into playlist-based teaching series  

---

## 13. Progression Standards

To remain in good standing, students must:

- Complete required coursework  
- Demonstrate consistent academic progress  
- Produce acceptable research outputs  
- Maintain repository activity  

---

## 14. Completion Criteria

To graduate, students must:

- Complete all required credits  
- Submit an approved thesis  
- Maintain a complete repository of work  
- Demonstrate mastery of program outcomes  

---

## 15. Program Identity

The MTS program is not designed for passive learning.

It is designed to produce:

- Thinkers  
- Builders  
- Researchers  
- System architects  

Students are expected to contribute to the development of theological knowledge systems, not simply study them.
