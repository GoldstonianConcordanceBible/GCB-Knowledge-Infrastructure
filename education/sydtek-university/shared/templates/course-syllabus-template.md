## GitHub Evidence Requirement

In GitHub-based courses, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repository visibility may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

Students must demonstrate academic work through appropriate repository evidence, which may include:

- commits
- milestone updates
- artifact development
- documentation
- linked reflections
- tagged submissions

Credit is awarded for verified learning and documented performance, not for visibility alone.

---

## Public and Private Pathways

Where a course includes public discussion, public peer review, or open artifact publication, students must still have access to an equivalent rigorous pathway when public visibility is not appropriate due to privacy, age, accessibility, safety, or family considerations.

Equivalent pathways must preserve:

- the same learning outcomes
- similar workload
- critique and revision expectations
- rubric-based evaluation
- documentation quality

## Open Discussion Pedagogy Alignment

Where applicable, this course may use Open Discussion Pedagogy, which treats evidence-linked discourse as a structured site of learning.
