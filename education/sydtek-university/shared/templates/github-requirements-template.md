## Purpose

---

## Core Requirement

In GitHub-based courses, academic credit is awarded for verifiable documented work aligned to course outcomes.

Repositories may be public or private unless otherwise stated, but authorized academic reviewers must be able to inspect required evidence.

---

## Minimum Expectations

Students should provide:

- organized repository structure
- meaningful commit history
- clear file naming
- final deliverables in identifiable locations
- required reflections or documentation
- linked submission information where required

---

## Public and Private Pathways

Public repositories may support:

- portfolio development
- open critique
- public scholarship habits

Private repositories remain acceptable when allowed, provided faculty can review the required evidence.

---

## Review Principle

Credit is awarded for documented learning and demonstrable progression, not for repository visibility alone.
