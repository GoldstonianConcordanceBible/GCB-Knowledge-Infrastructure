# Peer Review and Equivalent Pathways Policy
## SydTek University

SydTek University values critique, revision, and scholarly exchange as essential parts of learning.

## Public Peer Review

Some courses may incorporate public or semi-public peer review as part of the learning design.

Where used, peer review should be:

- prompt-driven
- evidence-linked
- moderated
- rubric-aware
- documented

## Equivalent Alternative

Students who do not participate in public peer review must be offered an equivalent rigorous pathway when appropriate.

Equivalent alternatives may include:

- instructor review
- closed cohort review
- anonymized review
- private critique groups
- documented written response cycles

## Academic Principle

The academic requirement is not exposure.

The academic requirement is disciplined critique engagement, revision, and evidence of learning.

## Documentation

All peer review activities, whether public or private, should be documentable through:

- linked artifacts
- response memos
- revision logs
- rubric records
- evidence dossiers
