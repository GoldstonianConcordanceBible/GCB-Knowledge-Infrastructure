# Public and Private Repository Pathways
## SydTek University

SydTek University recognizes that students may complete GitHub-based academic work through either public or private repositories.

## Principle

Public visibility may strengthen authentic discourse, peer engagement, and portfolio development.

However, public visibility must not be treated as a universal requirement for credit where safety, age, privacy, accessibility, family preference, or other legitimate concerns make a private pathway more appropriate.

## Public Pathway

In a public pathway, students may:

- publish repositories openly
- participate in visible peer discourse
- receive public-facing critique where course-approved
- develop portfolio-ready artifacts

## Private Pathway

In a private pathway, students may:

- complete the same academic work in private repositories
- submit evidence directly to instructors
- participate in institutionally managed critique spaces
- preserve privacy while meeting the same academic standards

## Institutional Standard

Both pathways must be academically equivalent in:

- learning outcomes
- evidence requirements
- rubric expectations
- instructor oversight
- documentation quality

## Review Access

Students using private repositories must provide appropriate review access to authorized faculty or institutional reviewers.

## Parent and Student Choice

When legally or educationally appropriate, public versus private repository selection may be left to the student or parent/guardian, provided that course requirements and evidence standards are still met.
