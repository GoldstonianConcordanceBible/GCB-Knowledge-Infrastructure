# Instructor Onboarding Guide
## SydTek University

This guide introduces instructors to the SydTek University instructional and evidence model.

---

## Core Teaching Principle

SydTek University evaluates learning through documented academic work aligned to defined outcomes.

In many courses, GitHub serves as a primary evidence environment.

Credit is awarded for verifiable documented work, not for visibility alone.

---

## Institutional Teaching Framework

Instructors should understand the following institutional foundations:

- GitHub evidence policy
- public/private repository pathways
- Open Discussion Pedagogy
- evidence dossier framework
- audit packet framework
- Mirror → Water → Fire instructional alignment

---

## Instructor Responsibilities

Instructors are expected to:

- align assignments to outcomes
- communicate expectations clearly
- apply rubrics consistently
- preserve required evidence
- review authorship and progression where needed
- support equivalent rigorous pathways where appropriate

---

## GitHub-Based Review Responsibility

In GitHub-based courses, instructors should be prepared to review:

- repository organization
- commit progression
- documentation quality
- milestone completion
- linked reflections
- final artifact integrity

---

## Public and Private Pathways

Instructors must remember:

- public work may strengthen authenticity and portfolio development
- private pathways remain acceptable where appropriate
- equivalent rigor must be preserved
- the academic standard is documented learning under faculty oversight

---

## Good Practice

Strong SydTek instruction should include:

- clear prompts
- evidence-linked assignments
- revision opportunities
- preserved documentation
- transparent evaluation
