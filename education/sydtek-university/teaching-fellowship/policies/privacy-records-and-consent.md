# SydTek University Teaching Fellowship
## Privacy, Records, and Consent Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes how SydTek University collects, uses, stores, protects, and governs:
- student educational records,
- instructional recordings,
- fellowship portfolios,
- and any data involving minors or adult learners.

Because the Teaching Fellowship relies on **recorded instruction, submitted artifacts, and digital platforms**, this policy ensures:
- privacy protection,
- legal and ethical compliance,
- controlled data usage,
- and accreditation-ready documentation practices.

---

# SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- SydTek University students,
- SydTek Scholars (K–12 learners),
- faculty supervisors,
- site supervisors,
- staff,
- and any system or platform used to store or transmit academic records.

---

# DEFINITIONS

## Educational Record
Any record directly related to a student and maintained by SydTek University or a party acting on its behalf.

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Information that can identify a student, including:
- name,
- image,
- voice,
- contact information,
- identifiers,
- or any combination of data that can reasonably identify the student.

## Fellowship Portfolio
A structured submission of evidence used to evaluate academic performance, including:
- lesson plans,
- recordings,
- student work,
- reflections,
- assessments,
- and supervisor feedback.

---

# CORE PRIVACY PRINCIPLES

SydTek University operates under the following principles:

1. **Minimum Necessary Use**  
   Only the minimum amount of student information required for academic purposes should be collected or shared.

2. **Purpose Limitation**  
   Data may only be used for legitimate institutional purposes (instruction, assessment, supervision, accreditation).

3. **Access Control**  
   Access to records must be restricted to authorized individuals.

4. **Security and Integrity**  
   Records must be protected from unauthorized access, alteration, or loss.

5. **Transparency**  
   Students and parents must be informed about how data is used.

---

# CONSENT REQUIREMENTS

## Parental Consent (Minors)

Before collecting or using any of the following, SydTek must obtain appropriate consent:
- video recordings of minors,
- student work used in fellowship portfolios,
- online instruction involving recording or monitoring,
- sharing of student artifacts beyond instructional use.

Consent must clearly state:
- what data is collected,
- how it will be used,
- who will have access,
- and whether it may be retained or reused.

## Adult Student Consent

Fellowship participants must consent to:
- submission of their work for evaluation,
- review by faculty and supervisors,
- retention of academic records,
- and use of anonymized data for institutional improvement.

# RECORD CATEGORIES

## 1. Academic Records
- grades,
- evaluations,
- transcripts,
- faculty feedback.

## 2. Instructional Artifacts
- lesson plans,
- curriculum documents,
- assessments,
- reflection journals.

## 3. Instructional Recordings
- video or audio of teaching sessions,
- observation recordings,
- online session captures.

## 4. Student Work Samples (Minors)
- assignments,
- written responses,
- participation evidence.

Each category must be governed by access, retention, and usage rules.

---

# USE OF RECORDINGS

## Permitted Uses
Recordings may be used for:
- academic evaluation,
- supervision,
- training,
- internal review,
- accreditation evidence.

## Restricted Uses
Recordings may NOT be:
- publicly posted,
- used for marketing,
- shared externally,
- or redistributed,

unless:
- explicit consent has been obtained,
- and institutional approval has been granted.

## Redaction Requirement
Where possible:
- faces,
- names,
- and identifying details

should be minimized or redacted in submitted materials.

---

# ACCESS CONTROL

Access to records is limited to:

- Faculty Supervisors (evaluation)
- Approved Site Supervisors (observation)
- Program Leadership (oversight)
- Registrar (official records)
- Authorized IT systems (storage)

Students may access their own records but not those of others.

Parents may access minor-related records as permitted by policy and law.

---

# DATA STORAGE REQUIREMENTS

SydTek University must ensure that:

- records are stored in secure systems,
- access is authenticated and logged,
- backups are maintained,
- and systems meet reasonable security standards.

Approved storage may include:
- institutional repositories,
- secured cloud systems,
- controlled GitHub structures (non-public for sensitive data).

Public repositories must NOT contain:
- identifiable student data,
- protected educational records,
- or sensitive fellowship evidence.

---

# GITHUB AND PUBLIC DATA RULE

Because SydTek uses GitHub:

## Allowed:
- curriculum structures,
- anonymized lesson plans,
- general frameworks,
- non-identifiable examples.

## Not Allowed:
- student names,
- identifiable recordings,
- raw assessment data tied to individuals,
- private evaluation records.

All public content must be **de-identified and reviewed** before release.

---

# DATA RETENTION POLICY

The institution should define retention timelines for:

- academic records (long-term retention),
- fellowship portfolios (defined retention period),
- recordings (shorter retention unless needed),
- incident reports (per policy and law).

Records should be:
- retained only as long as necessary,
- securely archived or deleted when no longer needed.

---

# DATA DELETION AND REQUESTS

Students or parents may request:
- access to records,
- correction of inaccurate data,
- or clarification of how data is used.

Deletion requests must be balanced against:
- academic recordkeeping requirements,
- accreditation documentation,
- and legal obligations.

---

# DATA BREACH RESPONSE

In the event of unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure:

SydTek University must:
1. identify the scope of the breach,
2. secure affected systems,
3. document the incident,
4. notify appropriate parties when required,
5. and implement corrective actions.

---

# THIRD-PARTY SYSTEMS

If SydTek uses third-party platforms (video, LMS, cloud tools), the institution must ensure:
- data protection standards are acceptable,
- access is controlled,
- and data is not misused.

Third-party tools must align with institutional privacy expectations.

---

# TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

All participants must receive training on:
- privacy expectations,
- data handling,
- recording rules,
- consent requirements,
- and secure usage of platforms.

---

# FACULTY AND SUPERVISOR RESPONSIBILITY

Faculty and supervisors must:
- protect student data,
- avoid unauthorized sharing,
- ensure evidence submissions are compliant,
- and report any privacy concerns.

---

# PRIVACY AND ACADEMIC CREDIT

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
- removal of submitted evidence,
- requirement for resubmission,
- disciplinary action,
- or denial of academic credit.

---

# ANONYMIZATION STANDARD

Before using any data for:
- publication,
- research,
- training,
- or public sharing,

the institution must ensure:
- removal of identifying details,
- aggregation where appropriate,
- and protection against re-identification.

---

# POLICY REVIEW

This policy should be reviewed regularly to ensure alignment with:
- legal requirements,
- accreditation expectations,
- technological changes,
- and institutional growth.

---

# FOUNDATIONAL PRIVACY STATEMENT

SydTek University recognizes that educational data—especially involving minors—is not a byproduct of the system but a responsibility.

All fellowship data must be handled with **care, control, and accountability**.

Privacy is not optional. It is foundational.
