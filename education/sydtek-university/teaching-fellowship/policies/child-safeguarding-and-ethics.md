# SydTek University Teaching Fellowship
## Child Safeguarding and Ethics Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the child safeguarding, ethical conduct, and risk management requirements for all SydTek University Teaching Fellowship activities conducted within SydTek Scholars or any affiliated instructional environment involving minors.

Because the Teaching Fellowship places adult learners in educational relationships with children, SydTek University must maintain clear institutional safeguards that protect minors, support parents, guide faculty and supervisors, and preserve the integrity of academic and instructional practice.

This policy applies to:
- fellowship students,
- faculty supervisors,
- site supervisors,
- staff,
- volunteers acting in approved instructional roles,
- and any approved partner or affiliate participating in SydTek Scholars instructional activity.

## Policy Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:
- protect minors from abuse, neglect, exploitation, and misconduct,
- establish clear behavioral standards for adults working with children,
- define supervision and reporting expectations,
- protect student privacy and confidentiality,
- reduce institutional risk,
- and ensure that fellowship-based teaching remains appropriate, ethical, and mission-aligned.

## Institutional Duty

SydTek University and SydTek Scholars share a duty to provide a safe learning environment for minors.

SydTek University retains final institutional authority for:
- policy approval,
- training requirements,
- background screening standards,
- supervision requirements,
- reporting expectations,
- and academic control over fellowship participation.

SydTek Scholars functions as the educational environment in which these safeguards are operationalized during K–12 instruction.

## Foundational Rule

No adult may participate in instructional, observational, mentoring, supervisory, or fellowship activity involving minors unless the adult has satisfied all required safeguarding conditions established by SydTek University.

## Required Safeguarding Conditions

Before working with minors, all applicable participants must complete:
- application or appointment approval,
- identity verification,
- background screening as required by institutional policy and applicable law,
- safeguarding and conduct training,
- documentation of policy acknowledgment,
- and any additional orientation required by the program.

No exceptions should be made for informal volunteers, ministry familiarity, or prior relationship to the institution.

## Standards of Ethical Conduct

All adults participating in the Teaching Fellowship must:
- treat minors with dignity, care, and respect,
- maintain appropriate educational boundaries,
- avoid favoritism, manipulation, intimidation, or coercion,
- communicate in ways that are age-appropriate and professional,
- act consistently with SydTek University’s Judeo-Christian mission,
- and comply with all institutional safeguarding procedures.

## Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is prohibited:
- abuse, neglect, or exploitation of any child,
- physical intimidation or threatening behavior,
- one-on-one unsupervised activity in prohibited settings,
- inappropriate private messaging with minors outside approved channels,
- sharing sexually explicit, degrading, or abusive content,
- retaliation against a child or family for raising concerns,
- unauthorized photography, recording, or disclosure of student information,
- and any conduct that violates applicable law or institutional policy.

Any participant who engages in prohibited conduct is subject to removal, reporting, disciplinary action, and possible referral to law enforcement or child protection authorities.

## Adult-to-Minor Boundary Rules

Adults serving in SydTek Scholars or the Teaching Fellowship must maintain clear boundaries.

### Boundary expectations include:
- using approved instructional or communication channels,
- limiting contact to educationally appropriate purposes,
- avoiding secretive communication,
- avoiding transportation of minors unless expressly authorized,
- avoiding isolated meetings unless policy conditions are met,
- and ensuring visibility and accountability in instructional relationships.

## Two-Adult / Observable Environment Rule

Whenever feasible, instruction, tutoring, mentoring, or fellowship-related interaction with minors should occur:
- in observable settings,
- through recorded or documented virtual environments,
- or in the presence of another approved adult.

If one-on-one instructional interaction is permitted for academic reasons, the institution must define:
- when it is allowed,
- how it is documented,
- what visibility controls are required,
- and what parental notice or consent is necessary.

## Digital Instruction and Online Communication

Because SydTek Scholars may use online and hybrid delivery, digital safeguarding rules are required.

Approved adults must:
- use institutionally approved platforms or approved documented channels,
- avoid disappearing-message applications for instructional communication,
- avoid private or unlogged contact outside approved processes,
- maintain professional tone and purpose,
- and comply with recording, privacy, and consent requirements.

## Recordings, Photos, and Student Privacy

Any recording, photo, video, screenshot, or digital artifact involving minors must be handled according to institutional privacy rules and applicable law.

Such materials may only be created, retained, shared, or published when:
- there is a legitimate educational or institutional purpose,
- required notice and consent have been obtained,
- access is limited appropriately,
- and retention is governed by institutional policy.

Unauthorized capture or distribution of a minor’s image, voice, work, or identifying information is prohibited.

## Educational Records and Confidentiality

Student information must be treated as confidential and handled only for legitimate institutional purposes.

Adults participating in the fellowship may access student-related information only to the extent necessary for:
- instruction,
- supervision,
- assessment,
- safeguarding,
- or authorized institutional review.

No participant may disclose confidential student information casually, publicly, or for promotional purposes without proper authorization.

## Mandatory Reporting and Incident Reporting

All fellowship participants must immediately report suspected:
- abuse,
- neglect,
- exploitation,
- grooming,
- serious boundary violations,
- or other conduct that may place a minor at risk.

Reports must be made according to:
- institutional reporting procedures,
- applicable mandatory reporting laws,
- and emergency protocols where immediate danger is present.

The institution must provide clear written instructions identifying:
- who receives internal reports,
- when external authorities must be notified,
- and how documentation must be preserved.

## Non-Retaliation Rule

No person may be penalized, intimidated, or retaliated against for making a good-faith safeguarding report or participating in an investigation.

Retaliation is itself a policy violation.

## Training Requirements

Safeguarding training must be completed before contact with minors begins and renewed on a recurring schedule set by the institution.

Training should include:
- recognizing abuse and grooming behaviors,
- appropriate boundaries,
- reporting obligations,
- digital safety,
- confidentiality,
- and institution-specific procedures.

Completion records must be retained by the institution.

## Background Screening

SydTek University should adopt written screening procedures for all adults who work with minors in recurring, supervisory, or instructional roles.

Screening should address:
- the scope of required checks,
- renewal intervals,
- disqualification criteria,
- documentation standards,
- and response procedures if concerns are identified.

Screening alone does not replace supervision.

## Parental Awareness and Consent

Because SydTek Scholars serves minors, the institution should maintain clear parent-facing practices addressing:
- the nature of instructional participation,
- online platform use,
- communication methods,
- recording practices,
- student work collection,
- and any required permissions or consents.

Parents should know who is teaching, how instruction is supervised, and how concerns may be reported.

## Use of Student Work and Instructional Artifacts

If fellowship students submit evidence that includes student work or instructional recordings, the institution must ensure:
- only the minimum necessary student information is included,
- personally identifiable information is limited or redacted when appropriate,
- access is restricted to authorized reviewers,
- and publication is prohibited unless expressly authorized.

## Social Media and Public Content

Because SydTek may use digital content ecosystems, no participant may publicly post:
- student names,
- student records,
- identifiable images or videos of minors,
- private family information,
- or instructional incidents involving minors,

unless the institution has approved the disclosure and all required permissions have been obtained.

Educational enthusiasm does not override privacy obligations.

## Faculty, Supervisor, and Site Responsibility

Faculty supervisors, site supervisors, and program leaders must:
- monitor compliance,
- reinforce professional boundaries,
- document concerns,
- escalate incidents promptly,
- and ensure that fellowship participation stops immediately when child safety is at risk.

## Removal from Fellowship Activity

A participant may be suspended or removed from fellowship activity involving minors when:
- safeguarding requirements are incomplete,
- concerning conduct is reported,
- supervision conditions are violated,
- policy compliance is uncertain,
- or institutional leadership determines that continued participation presents risk.

Temporary removal pending review is permitted when child safety or institutional integrity requires immediate action.

## Investigations and Institutional Response

SydTek University should maintain written procedures for:
- documenting allegations,
- preserving evidence,
- limiting disclosure to those with a need to know,
- coordinating with site leadership,
- making required external reports,
- and determining interim and final actions.

Internal review does not replace legally required external reporting.

## Ethics in Faith-Based Instruction

Because SydTek University is a Judeo-Christian institution, faith-based teaching must be conducted with integrity and care.

No participant may use theological authority, scripture, institutional status, or spiritual language to:
- manipulate,
- shame,
- isolate,
- coerce,
- or silence a child.

Faith-based education must remain educationally appropriate, ethically grounded, and consistent with institutional safeguarding obligations.

## Child Safeguarding and Academic Credit

No student may earn or retain fellowship credit for instructional activity involving minors if that activity was conducted in violation of this policy.

Academic credit is contingent not only on teaching performance, but also on compliance with child safeguarding and ethical conduct requirements.

## Record Retention

The institution should retain, according to written policy:
- training records,
- background screening records as permitted,
- policy acknowledgments,
- incident reports,
- investigative documentation,
- and supervision records tied to safeguarding concerns.

Access to such records must be restricted appropriately.

## Annual Review

This policy should be reviewed regularly and updated as needed to reflect:
- changes in law,
- accreditor expectations,
- institutional growth,
- online learning practices,
- and lessons learned from implementation.

## Foundational Safeguarding Statement

SydTek University recognizes that any educational model involving minors must place child safety, ethical conduct, and institutional accountability above convenience, informality, or scale.

No academic flywheel is legitimate unless children are protected within it.
