# Chunk 2 Implementation Notes

## Why this file is accreditation-ready

This credit-mapping framework is built to align with three core external expectations:

1. **Federal credit-hour expectations**  
   The federal definition in 34 CFR 600.2 recognizes a credit hour as an amount of work represented in intended learning outcomes and verified by evidence of student achievement, including approximately one hour of direct instruction and two hours of out-of-class work each week for about fifteen weeks, or an equivalent amount of work for other academic activities.

2. **ABHE expectations for competency-based and credit-bearing programs**  
   ABHE requires institutions to explain how they determine credit hours or credit-hour equivalents, especially in competency-based education, and to show that assigned credit is warranted and consistent with general higher education practice. ABHE also requires measurable outcomes and structured assessment processes.

3. **Cognia expectations for continuous improvement and effective teaching and learning systems**  
   Cognia frames accreditation around research-based performance standards, measurable improvement, and institutional systems that support learner growth and instructional quality.
