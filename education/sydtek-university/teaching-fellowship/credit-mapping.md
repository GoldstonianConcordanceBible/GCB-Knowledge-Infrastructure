# SydTek University Teaching Fellowship
## Credit Mapping Framework

## Purpose

This document defines how the SydTek University Teaching Fellowship maps supervised teaching, curriculum work, assessment practice, mentorship, and research activity into institutionally defensible academic credit. It is designed to support accreditation review, internal governance, faculty oversight, and transcripting decisions.

This framework does not treat volunteer service as automatic academic credit. Credit may only be awarded when fellowship activity is:
- aligned to published learning outcomes,
- appropriate to the academic level,
- supervised by qualified faculty,
- documented through direct evidence,
- and evaluated through approved rubrics and institutional processes.

## Governing Principle

SydTek University assigns credit only when the amount and level of student work are equivalent to higher education expectations and are supported by documented learning outcomes, assessment evidence, and institutional review.

## Credit-Hour Foundation

For standard academic programs, SydTek University should anchor its framework to the commonly accepted federal definition of a credit hour:

- one hour of classroom or direct faculty instruction plus a minimum of two hours of out-of-class student work each week for approximately fifteen weeks, or
- an equivalent amount of work established by the institution for other academic activities.

This principle should govern fellowship credit even when the fellowship includes applied teaching, online delivery, supervised practice, curriculum design, or competency-based assessment.

## Institutional Rule

No teaching fellowship activity may be transcripted unless all of the following are present:
1. a course or approved academic requirement to which the activity is attached,
2. published learning outcomes,
3. faculty or approved academic supervisor oversight,
4. documented evidence of student work,
5. rubric-based evaluation,
6. and an approved institutional credit mapping.

## Two Approved Credit Models

SydTek University may use either or both of the following models.

### 1. Seat-Time-Equivalent Credit Model
This model is used when the institution wants fellowship work to map directly to semester credit hours.

### 2. Competency-Based Credit Model
This model is used when the institution awards credit upon demonstrated mastery of defined competencies, while still maintaining a defensible relationship between competencies, workload, and academic level.

## Level-Specific Credit Expectations

## Undergraduate Fellowship Credit

Undergraduate fellowship credit is designed for foundational academic formation in teaching, theology, and instructional practice.

### Typical Undergraduate Credit Uses
- introductory practicum,
- lesson planning,
- foundations of faith-based pedagogy,
- introductory classroom assessment,
- biblical literacy in teaching,
- reflective teaching journals.

### Suggested Undergraduate Credit Standard
One 3-credit undergraduate practicum should normally include work such as:
- supervised instructional contact,
- preparation and lesson design,
- grading or student feedback,
- reflective writing,
- faculty review,
- and final portfolio submission.

### Undergraduate Example Mapping
A 3-credit fellowship practicum may include:
- 15 hours of direct seminar or supervision,
- 30 hours of supervised teaching or observation,
- 45 hours of planning, grading, and preparation,
- 45 hours of reflection, revision, and portfolio development.

**Total: 135 hours of student work**

This keeps the practicum aligned with a standard semester-credit expectation.

## Graduate Fellowship Credit

Graduate fellowship credit is designed for advanced application, leadership, curriculum systems thinking, and instructional analysis.

### Typical Graduate Credit Uses
- curriculum development,
- assessment design,
- teacher coaching,
- theological integration across multiple units,
- instructional leadership,
- program improvement planning.

### Suggested Graduate Credit Standard
Graduate fellowship credit should require more depth, stronger writing, more analytical rigor, and a higher degree of independent responsibility than undergraduate credit.

### Graduate Example Mapping
A 3-credit graduate fellowship course may include:
- 15 hours of graduate seminar or faculty consultation,
- 25 hours of supervised teaching or coaching activity,
- 35 hours of curriculum design or revision,
- 25 hours of learner assessment analysis,
- 35 hours of research, synthesis, and formal written reflection.

**Total: 135 hours of student work**

The difference from undergraduate work is not merely time. It is the higher level of complexity, analysis, leadership, and theological integration.

## Doctoral Fellowship Credit

Doctoral fellowship credit is designed for original inquiry, systems design, supervised leadership, institutional research, and dissertation-aligned fieldwork.

### Typical Doctoral Credit Uses
- cohort supervision,
- fellowship model design,
- institutional effectiveness research,
- educational systems development,
- advanced theological-pedagogical theory,
- dissertation field research within SydTek Scholars or SydTek University.

### Suggested Doctoral Credit Standard
Doctoral credit should require advanced scholarship, professional-quality writing, original contribution, and clear evidence that the student is operating at a level beyond graduate professional practice.

### Doctoral Example Mapping
A 3-credit doctoral field-research or fellowship course may include:
- 10 hours of doctoral supervision,
- 20 hours of supervised leadership in the teaching fellowship,
- 30 hours of research design and data collection,
- 30 hours of analysis and interpretation,
- 45 hours of doctoral writing, revision, and formal reporting.

**Total: 135 hours of student work**

Doctoral work should also include stronger review for methodological quality, ethics, and contribution to institutional learning.

## Competency-Based Credit Mapping

If SydTek University uses a competency-based model, each fellowship course or requirement must identify:
- the competencies to be demonstrated,
- the evidence required,
- the evaluation standard,
- the faculty role in verification,
- and the institutional basis for assigning credit-hour equivalence.

### Undergraduate Competency Examples
- designs age-appropriate lesson plans,
- teaches with biblical and theological accuracy,
- uses introductory assessment tools appropriately,
- reflects on instructional strengths and weaknesses,
- responds constructively to supervision.

### Graduate Competency Examples
- designs coherent curriculum sequences,
- interprets student assessment evidence,
- mentors or coaches novice teachers,
- integrates theology with pedagogy at a systems level,
- recommends instructional improvements based on evidence.

### Doctoral Competency Examples
- evaluates and improves fellowship systems,
- produces original research tied to educational practice,
- supervises instructional cohorts effectively,
- synthesizes theology, pedagogy, and institutional design,
- contributes new knowledge or models for Judeo-Christian education.

## Minimum Evidence Required for Credit

At every level, fellowship credit should require a portfolio that includes, as applicable:
- approved learning contract or fellowship plan,
- lesson plans,
- instructional recordings or observation records,
- curriculum artifacts,
- assessment instruments,
- student work samples where permitted,
- reflective writing,
- supervisor feedback,
- final faculty evaluation,
- and a summative portfolio review.

## Distinction Between Experience and Credit

Experience alone is not credit.

A participant may have many hours of teaching experience, but SydTek University should only award credit when that experience is transformed into evaluated academic work through:
- faculty oversight,
- evidence submission,
- alignment to outcomes,
- and institutional assessment.

## Prior Learning and Advanced Standing

If SydTek University later develops a prior learning assessment process, it may evaluate previously completed teaching experience for advanced standing or credit recommendations. Any such process must:
- use published criteria,
- evaluate documented evidence,
- verify that learning is college-level,
- and maintain academic integrity and consistency across students.

Prior experience should never bypass institutional review simply because the applicant has served in ministry, homeschooling, or teaching roles.

## Supervision Load and Faculty Responsibility

Credit mapping must also account for faculty responsibility. The institution should not scale fellowship credit faster than it can provide:
- qualified supervision,
- timely feedback,
- reliable evaluation,
- and recordkeeping capacity.

If supervision is thin or inconsistent, the credit model becomes difficult to defend.

## Transcripting Rule

Credit should appear on the transcript only when:
- the fellowship has been attached to an approved course, practicum, residency, internship, or dissertation field requirement,
- the student has completed all required artifacts,
- the faculty evaluator has submitted a final determination,
- and the registrar or designated academic authority has recorded the result through approved procedures.

## Institutional Control Rule

Because SydTek Scholars functions as the practicum environment and SydTek University functions as the degree-granting authority, all final decisions regarding academic credit must remain under SydTek University academic control.

SydTek Scholars mentors, parents, or site coordinators may contribute observational feedback, but they should not independently award university credit.

## Quality Assurance Checklist

Before approving a fellowship credit map, SydTek University should verify:
- learning outcomes are published,
- academic level is clearly defined,
- workload is documented,
- rubric criteria are approved,
- supervision roles are assigned,
- portfolio requirements are clear,
- transcripting procedures are defined,
- and institutional records can support future accreditation review.

## Foundational Credit Statement

SydTek University awards fellowship credit not for activity alone, but for **verified academic learning demonstrated through supervised practice, assessed evidence, and institutionally approved credit mapping**.
