# SydTek University Teaching Fellowship
## Supervision Framework

## Purpose

This document defines the supervision structure for the SydTek University Teaching Fellowship. It establishes who is authorized to supervise, how supervision is conducted, how evaluation authority is maintained, and how supervision supports accreditation standards.

Supervision is the mechanism that ensures:
- academic integrity,
- consistent evaluation,
- student support and development,
- and institutional control over credit-awarding decisions.

No fellowship credit may be awarded without documented supervision.

---

# SUPERVISION MODEL OVERVIEW

The SydTek Teaching Fellowship uses a **three-layer supervision model**:

1. **Faculty Supervisor (Primary Academic Authority)**
2. **Site Supervisor (SydTek Scholars Environment)**
3. **Program Oversight (Institutional Control Layer)**

Each layer has distinct responsibilities and must not be conflated.

---

# 1. FACULTY SUPERVISOR

## Role

The Faculty Supervisor is the **final academic authority** responsible for:
- evaluating student performance,
- applying rubrics,
- determining competency achievement,
- and approving or denying academic credit.

## Minimum Responsibilities

The Faculty Supervisor must:
- approve the fellowship plan before activity begins,
- review lesson plans and instructional materials,
- evaluate submitted evidence,
- apply official rubrics,
- provide written feedback,
- determine final course or fellowship outcome,
- and submit results for transcripting.

## Qualification Requirements

Faculty Supervisors should:
- hold a degree at or above the level of the program they supervise,
- demonstrate subject-matter expertise (theology, education, or related field),
- be approved by SydTek University through formal processes,
- and be trained in rubric use and fellowship evaluation standards.

## Non-Delegation Rule

Final academic decisions **may not be delegated** to:
- parents,
- volunteers,
- site coordinators,
- or unapproved mentors.

Only authorized faculty or formally approved academic supervisors may assign final grades or credit decisions.

---

# 2. SITE SUPERVISOR (SYDTEK SCHOLARS)

## Role

The Site Supervisor operates within SydTek Scholars and provides **direct observation and contextual feedback**.

This role supports, but does not replace, academic authority.

## Responsibilities

The Site Supervisor may:
- observe teaching sessions,
- document classroom performance,
- provide formative feedback,
- verify participation and engagement,
- and submit observational reports.

## Limitations

The Site Supervisor may NOT:
- assign academic credit,
- override faculty evaluation,
- determine final grades,
- or independently validate competency achievement.

## Acceptable Site Supervisors

Site Supervisors may include:
- experienced educators,
- program coordinators,
- trained homeschool leaders,
- or approved instructional mentors.

All Site Supervisors must be:
- vetted by SydTek University,
- trained in observation expectations,
- and aligned with institutional policies.

---

# 3. PROGRAM OVERSIGHT (INSTITUTIONAL CONTROL)

## Role

Program Oversight ensures that the Teaching Fellowship operates consistently, fairly, and in alignment with accreditation expectations.

## Responsibilities

Program Oversight must:
- approve supervision structures,
- verify faculty qualifications,
- audit evaluation consistency,
- ensure documentation is complete,
- review program effectiveness,
- and enforce institutional policies.

This layer may be fulfilled by:
- program directors,
- academic deans,
- or designated institutional leadership.

---

# SUPERVISION PROCESS FLOW

## Step 1: Fellowship Plan Approval
- Student submits fellowship plan
- Faculty Supervisor reviews and approves
- Learning outcomes and expectations are confirmed

## Step 2: Active Teaching and Observation
- Student teaches within SydTek Scholars
- Site Supervisor observes and documents
- Student collects evidence (videos, lesson plans, assessments)

## Step 3: Ongoing Feedback
- Site Supervisor provides formative feedback
- Faculty Supervisor may provide interim guidance
- Student revises and improves practice

## Step 4: Evidence Submission
- Student submits full portfolio
- Includes all required artifacts and documentation

## Step 5: Faculty Evaluation
- Faculty applies rubrics
- Reviews all evidence
- Determines competency achievement

## Step 6: Final Determination
- Faculty assigns final outcome
- Program verifies documentation
- Registrar records credit if approved

---

# MINIMUM SUPERVISION REQUIREMENTS

Each fellowship experience must include:

- at least one approved Faculty Supervisor,
- documented Site Supervisor involvement (where applicable),
- at least one formal observation record,
- documented feedback cycles,
- and final faculty evaluation.

Programs may require additional observations depending on level.

---

# OBSERVATION REQUIREMENTS

## Undergraduate
- minimum of 1–2 documented observations

## Graduate
- minimum of 2–3 documented observations
- may include peer or mentored teaching review

## Doctoral
- multiple observations and/or supervision of others
- must include leadership or systems-level evaluation

Observations may be conducted:
- in-person,
- through recorded video,
- or through approved digital platforms.

---

# DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

Supervision must be documented through:

- observation reports,
- feedback records,
- rubric evaluations,
- communication logs (if applicable),
- and final faculty determination.

All documentation must be retained according to institutional recordkeeping policies.

---

# SUPERVISION-TO-STUDENT RATIO GUIDELINE

To maintain quality:

- Undergraduate: 1 faculty per 15–25 students (with site support)
- Graduate: 1 faculty per 10–15 students
- Doctoral: 1 faculty per 5–10 students

If ratios exceed these ranges, additional supervisors should be assigned.

---

# TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

All supervisors must receive training on:
- competency framework,
- rubric use,
- evaluation standards,
- documentation expectations,
- and institutional policies.

Training completion should be documented.

---

# CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

Supervisors must avoid conflicts of interest.

They should not:
- supervise immediate family members without disclosure,
- evaluate students where bias may exist,
- or participate in evaluation where independence is compromised.

Conflicts must be disclosed and reassigned where necessary.

---

# ESCALATION AND APPEALS

Students must have access to:
- a clear appeal process,
- a secondary review if disputes arise,
- and institutional channels for resolving concerns.

Appeals should be reviewed by:
- a program director,
- department chair,
- or designated academic authority.

---

# QUALITY ASSURANCE

SydTek University must periodically review:
- supervision consistency,
- rubric application,
- student outcomes,
- and documentation completeness.

This ensures continuous improvement and accreditation readiness.

---

# SUPERVISION AUTHORITY STATEMENT

All academic credit decisions within the Teaching Fellowship remain under the authority of SydTek University.

SydTek Scholars provides the instructional environment and observational support, but it does not confer academic credit.

---

# FOUNDATIONAL SUPERVISION STATEMENT

Supervision is the mechanism by which SydTek University transforms teaching activity into **verified, evaluated, and academically defensible learning**.

Without supervision, there is no credit.
