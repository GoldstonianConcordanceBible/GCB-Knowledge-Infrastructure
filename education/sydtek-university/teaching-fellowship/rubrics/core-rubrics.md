# SydTek University Teaching Fellowship
## Core Rubrics

## Purpose

This document establishes the core rubric structure for evaluating students in the SydTek University Teaching Fellowship. These rubrics are used to determine whether students have demonstrated college-level, graduate-level, or doctoral-level achievement within supervised teaching, curriculum, assessment, and theological integration activities.

These rubrics support:
- consistency across evaluators,
- academic integrity,
- accreditation readiness,
- faculty oversight,
- and defensible credit-awarding decisions.

Rubrics must be applied to all fellowship experiences that carry academic credit.

## Rubric Design Principles

All SydTek University fellowship rubrics must be:
- aligned to published competencies,
- tied to direct evidence,
- appropriate to academic level,
- applied by qualified faculty or approved academic supervisors,
- and retained as part of the academic evidence record.

Rubrics do not replace faculty judgment. They structure and document it.

## Standard Performance Levels

Unless a program has approved alternatives, the following four-level performance scale should be used.

| Score | Level | General Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 4 | Exemplary | Performance exceeds academic expectations for the level and demonstrates strong independence, clarity, and quality. |
| 3 | Proficient | Performance meets academic expectations for the level and demonstrates competent, satisfactory achievement. |
| 2 | Developing | Performance partially meets expectations but shows notable weaknesses, inconsistency, or incomplete mastery. |
| 1 | Beginning | Performance does not yet meet expectations and requires substantial improvement or rework. |

## Passing Rule

For fellowship credit to be awarded:
- no essential category may receive a score of 1,
- the overall performance must meet the minimum threshold established by the program,
- and faculty must determine that the evidence demonstrates achievement at the appropriate academic level.

Suggested institutional minimums:
- Undergraduate: average of 2.5 or higher
- Graduate: average of 3.0 or higher
- Doctoral: average of 3.25 or higher

Programs may raise these thresholds but should not lower them without formal approval.

## Core Rubric Categories

The following categories apply across most fellowship experiences:

1. Pedagogy and Instruction
2. Theological Integration
3. Curriculum and Design
4. Assessment and Learner Feedback
5. Professional Practice and Reflection

Additional program-specific categories may be added where needed.

# RUBRIC 1: PEDAGOGY AND INSTRUCTION

## Category Purpose

This rubric evaluates the student’s ability to teach effectively, communicate clearly, structure learning, and engage learners in age-appropriate and mission-aligned instruction.

## Undergraduate Performance Descriptors

| Level | Descriptor |
| --- | --- |
| 4 | Delivers clear, well-structured, age-appropriate instruction with strong learner engagement and confident classroom leadership. |
| 3 | Delivers clear and organized instruction appropriate to learner level with adequate engagement and sound teaching practice. |
| 2 | Instruction is partially clear or partially organized, but pacing, engagement, or clarity is inconsistent. |
| 1 | Instruction is unclear, poorly organized, or not appropriate to the learner group. |

## Graduate Performance Descriptors

| Level | Descriptor |
| --- | --- |
| 4 | Demonstrates advanced instructional leadership, adapts teaching effectively, and applies a strong range of strategies based on learner needs and outcomes. |
| 3 | Demonstrates effective graduate-level instructional practice with clear adaptation, sound methods, and competent leadership. |
| 2 | Demonstrates some graduate-level skill, but adaptation, leadership, or instructional coherence is inconsistent. |
| 1 | Does not yet demonstrate the level of instructional effectiveness expected of graduate study. |

## Doctoral Performance Descriptors

| Level | Descriptor |
| --- | --- |
| 4 | Designs, leads, and evaluates instruction at a systems level, demonstrating original thinking, strong supervision capacity, and doctoral-level authority. |
| 3 | Demonstrates effective doctoral-level instructional leadership and sound systems awareness in teaching practice. |
| 2 | Shows partial doctoral-level performance, but leadership, evaluation, or systems integration is underdeveloped. |
| 1 | Does not demonstrate doctoral-level instructional leadership or evaluation capacity. |

# ESSENTIAL CATEGORY RULE

The following categories are essential and normally may not be failed if credit is to be awarded:
- Pedagogy and Instruction
- Theological Integration
- Professional Practice and Reflection

Programs may designate additional essential categories depending on course type.

If a student scores 1 in an essential category, faculty should normally require remediation, resubmission, or non-passing status.

---

# EVIDENCE ALIGNMENT RULE

Each rubric score must be tied to direct evidence, such as:
- lesson plans,
- recordings,
- observation notes,
- assessment tools,
- learner work,
- written reflections,
- curriculum documents,
- research memoranda,
- or supervisory reports.

Rubric scores may not be based on impressions alone.

---

# FACULTY FEEDBACK REQUIREMENT

For all fellowship evaluations, faculty or approved supervisors must provide:
- rubric scores,
- brief narrative feedback,
- strengths identified,
- areas for improvement,
- and final academic determination.

Narrative comments should explain why the student met, exceeded, or fell below expectations.

---

# LEVEL DIFFERENTIATION RULE

The same student activity should not automatically receive the same score across academic levels.

For example:
- acceptable undergraduate performance may be insufficient at the graduate level,
- acceptable graduate leadership may be insufficient at the doctoral level.

Faculty must evaluate according to the academic level of enrollment, not simply the presence of teaching activity.

---

# INTER-RATER CONSISTENCY RULE

To maintain fairness and accreditation defensibility, SydTek University should:
- train faculty and supervisors in rubric use,
- calibrate scoring through sample reviews,
- review scoring patterns periodically,
- and revise rubric language when necessary.

Rubric consistency is part of academic quality assurance.

---

# REMEDIATION RULE

If a student does not meet the required standard, the program may require:
- revision of lesson plans,
- additional teaching observations,
- stronger theological integration,
- revised assessments,
- deeper written reflection,
- or additional supervised practice.

Remediation should be documented and tied to the unmet rubric areas.
