# SydTek University Teaching Fellowship
## Competency Framework

## Purpose

This document defines the **competencies required for awarding academic credit** through the SydTek University Teaching Fellowship. It establishes measurable expectations across undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral levels and ensures that all fellowship credit is based on demonstrated academic learning, not participation alone.

This framework is designed to:
- support accreditation requirements,
- guide rubric development,
- standardize evaluation across faculty,
- and align teaching practice with SydTek University’s Judeo-Christian mission.

## Competency Model Overview

All fellowship competencies are organized into five domains:

1. **Pedagogy and Instruction**
2. **Theological Integration**
3. **Curriculum and Design**
4. **Assessment and Data Use**
5. **Professional Practice and Reflection**

Each domain increases in rigor across academic levels:
- Undergraduate → foundational
- Graduate → applied + analytical
- Doctoral → original + systems-level

---

# DOMAIN 1: PEDAGOGY AND INSTRUCTION

## Undergraduate Competencies
The student:
- delivers clear, age-appropriate instruction,
- demonstrates basic classroom management,
- communicates biblical concepts accurately at a foundational level,
- follows structured lesson plans,
- engages learners through appropriate instructional strategies.

## Graduate Competencies
The student:
- adapts instruction based on learner needs,
- integrates multiple instructional strategies,
- demonstrates leadership in instructional delivery,
- mentors or supports less experienced instructors,
- aligns pedagogy with learning objectives and theological themes.

## Doctoral Competencies
The student:
- designs and evaluates instructional systems,
- leads cohorts of instructors,
- develops new pedagogical models,
- analyzes instructional effectiveness across contexts,
- contributes original frameworks for faith-based education.

---

# DOMAIN 2: THEOLOGICAL INTEGRATION

## Undergraduate Competencies
The student:
- demonstrates accurate understanding of biblical content,
- integrates scripture into lessons appropriately,
- aligns instruction with SydTek’s Judeo-Christian worldview,
- introduces learners to foundational theological concepts.

## Graduate Competencies
The student:
- integrates theology across units and curriculum,
- explains doctrinal concepts with clarity and depth,
- evaluates theological alignment in instructional materials,
- connects biblical teaching to ethical and developmental outcomes.

## Doctoral Competencies
The student:
- develops advanced theological-educational frameworks,
- critiques and refines doctrinal integration models,
- produces original scholarship in theology and education,
- contributes to institutional theological positioning,
- advances frameworks such as the Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine within pedagogy.

---

# DOMAIN 3: CURRICULUM AND DESIGN

## Undergraduate Competencies
The student:
- creates structured lesson plans,
- aligns lessons with stated objectives,
- organizes instructional content logically,
- uses basic curriculum resources effectively.

## Graduate Competencies
The student:
- designs multi-week or multi-unit curriculum,
- aligns curriculum with learning outcomes and assessments,
- adapts curriculum for diverse learners,
- integrates interdisciplinary connections,
- evaluates and revises curriculum based on outcomes.

## Doctoral Competencies
The student:
- designs scalable educational systems,
- develops institutional-level curriculum frameworks,
- evaluates program effectiveness,
- leads curriculum innovation initiatives,
- contributes to long-term curriculum architecture within SydTek University.

---

# DOMAIN 4: ASSESSMENT AND DATA USE

## Undergraduate Competencies
The student:
- uses basic assessment tools (quizzes, discussions, assignments),
- provides feedback to learners,
- identifies whether learning objectives are met,
- reflects on student performance.

## Graduate Competencies
The student:
- designs and implements assessment systems,
- analyzes student performance data,
- adjusts instruction based on evidence,
- evaluates assessment validity and alignment,
- supports other instructors in assessment practices.

## Doctoral Competencies
The student:
- develops institutional assessment frameworks,
- conducts data-driven program evaluation,
- leads assessment strategy across cohorts,
- contributes to institutional effectiveness reporting,
- integrates assessment data into research and system design.

---

# DOMAIN 5: PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE AND REFLECTION

## Undergraduate Competencies
The student:
- demonstrates reliability and professionalism,
- responds to feedback constructively,
- reflects on teaching experiences,
- identifies areas for improvement,
- maintains ethical conduct in teaching environments.

## Graduate Competencies
The student:
- demonstrates instructional leadership,
- mentors or supports other teachers,
- reflects critically on practice,
- contributes to program improvement,
- engages in ongoing professional development.

## Doctoral Competencies
The student:
- leads teams or cohorts within the fellowship,
- contributes to institutional strategy,
- produces scholarly reflection and research,
- models ethical leadership,
- advances the mission and long-term vision of SydTek University.

# COMPETENCY VALIDATION REQUIREMENTS

A competency is only considered achieved when:
1. evidence is submitted,
2. evidence aligns with the competency definition,
3. a rubric is applied,
4. a qualified faculty member evaluates performance,
5. and the result is documented within institutional records.

---

# REQUIRED EVIDENCE TYPES

Evidence may include:
- lesson plans,
- curriculum documents,
- teaching recordings,
- assessment tools,
- student work samples (where permitted),
- reflection papers,
- supervisor observations,
- mentorship logs,
- research papers (graduate/doctoral),
- dissertation components (doctoral).

---

# RUBRIC ALIGNMENT RULE

Each competency must be mapped to a rubric that includes:
- clear performance levels,
- defined evaluation criteria,
- consistency across evaluators,
- and alignment with academic level expectations.

No competency should be evaluated without an approved rubric.

---

# FACULTY RESPONSIBILITY

Faculty must:
- verify evidence authenticity,
- apply rubrics consistently,
- provide written feedback,
- determine competency achievement,
- and approve or deny credit.

Faculty decisions must be documented and auditable.

---

# COMPETENCY STACKING MODEL

Competencies may be grouped into:
- course-level requirements,
- practicum requirements,
- residency milestones,
- and dissertation expectations.

Students progress by demonstrating increasing mastery across stacked competencies.

---

# INTEGRATION WITH CREDIT MAPPING

Competencies must map to:
- credit hours (seat-time equivalent), or
- credit equivalency (competency-based model).

No competency framework may operate independently of the institutional credit model.

---

# QUALITY CONTROL

To maintain academic integrity, SydTek University must ensure:
- competencies are not vague or subjective,
- evaluation standards are consistent,
- faculty are trained in rubric use,
- documentation is retained,
- and periodic review updates competencies as needed.

---

# THEOLOGICAL ALIGNMENT STATEMENT

All competencies must align with SydTek University’s Judeo-Christian foundation and may incorporate:
- biblical literacy,
- theological reasoning,
- ethical formation,
- and doctrinal integration.

This alignment must be demonstrated through **academic work and evaluation**, not assumed.

---

# FOUNDATIONAL COMPETENCY STATEMENT

SydTek University awards fellowship credit based on **demonstrated mastery of defined competencies**, verified through evidence, evaluated through faculty oversight, and aligned with institutional academic standards.

Competency—not participation—is the basis of academic credit.
